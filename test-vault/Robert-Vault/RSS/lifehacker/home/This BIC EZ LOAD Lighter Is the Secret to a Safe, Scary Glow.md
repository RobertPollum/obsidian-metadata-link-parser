## Be smart about lighting your pumpkin’s candle

If you’re going old-school and using a real candle this year, make sure your pumpkin is sitting on a flat, stable surface so that it won’t tip over. Cut a good-sized hole in the gourd’s lid to let the heat from the candle escape, and put in a squatty votive candle, no tapered types. Use the [BIC EZ LOAD Lighter](https://zdcs.link/QOggZ7?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=BIC%20EZ%20LOAD%20Lighter&short_url=QOggZ7&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") to light the candle from a safe distance, and then place the lid ajar so that smoke can get out. Never leave a burning candle unattended.
