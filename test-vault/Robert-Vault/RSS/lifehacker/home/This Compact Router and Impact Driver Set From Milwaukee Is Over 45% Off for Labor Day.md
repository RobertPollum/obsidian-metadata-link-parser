## Why you might need a router and impact driver

A router allows you to round over corners on woodworking projects, trim your edges so they’re flush, and cut decorative profiles into the edges of your wood to make trim. You can also use routers for plunge-cutting grooves into the surface of wood or to trace patterns.

An impact driver allows you to drive screws into tougher material like two-by-fours more easily and without as much pressure. You can also use your impact driver as a drill if you have a drill bit designed to fit the hex, ¼ inch chuck.

I use the compact router to trim corners when I’m building a frame that has a plywood top and I like it because it’s more portable than the corded version I use for heavy-duty projects. It’s easier to control the router and concentrate on keeping it level with the surface I’m working on when I’m not dragging a cord behind me.

I use my impact driver daily and I find that it’s easier on my wrists than some other brands that aren’t balanced as well; it’s more comfortable to handle when I’m working for several hours and driving lots of screws.
