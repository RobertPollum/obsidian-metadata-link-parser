Gift shopping for the DIYer in your life can be a challenge. Tools are the obvious choice, but truly handy folks probably already have most, if not all, of the tools they need (in addition to a long list of tools they only needed _once_ but still hang onto). And even if they do need a specific tool, your chances of picking out the precise make and model they want are pretty slim.

But no matter [what level of DIYer](https://lifehacker.com/home/best-diy-home-improvement-projects-for-beginners) you're shopping for, they likely need other stuff [_besides_ tools](https://lifehacker.com/home/best-gifts-for-diyers-that-are-not-tools), from safety gear (which too many DIYers neglect), to cold-weather accessories, to the little extras that can make around-the-house projects a little easier, and maybe even more fun.

## Protective gear is a great option for DIY gifts

Safety is paramount—it’s terrifyingly easy to injure yourself while working with any kind of tool. Just as important is the “wear and tear” your body goes through while working with loud power tools and while contorting your body in ways nature never intended. A few safety and comfort essentials for the DIYer in your life include:

-   **Work boots:** Cold, wet feet make any job more miserable than it has to be, and if your DIY friend hasn’t dropped a hammer on their foot yet, don’t worry, they will. A pair of [waterproof, toe-protecting work boots](https://zdcs.link/QWELym?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=waterproof%2C%20toe-protecting%20work%20boots&short_url=QWELym&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is a great gift.
    
-   **Cut-resistant gloves:** Working with power saws or hand saws means risking a nasty cut. [Cut-resistant gloves](https://zdcs.link/QbMjGG?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Cut-resistant%20gloves&short_url=QbMjGG&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") protect those hands from all kinds of nicks and scrapes—and worse.
    
-   **Safety glasses:** The number of DIYers who think they don’t have to worry about eye injuries is, frankly, stunning. Do your handy loved one a favor and give them some [protective eyewear](https://zdcs.link/a5X83d?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=protective%20eyewear&short_url=a5X83d&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window").
    
-   **Knee pads:** I once spent a few days bent over in my crawl space, working, and my knees took a real beating. [Knee pads](https://zdcs.link/aADLA5?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Knee%20pads&short_url=aADLA5&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") may not be sexy, but they will be appreciated.
    
-   **Hearing protection:** Working with power tools can be hard on the ears. A pair of [noise-canceling, rechargeable headphones](https://zdcs.link/zn50Wy?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=noise-canceling%2C%20rechargeable%20headphones&short_url=zn50Wy&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") that play music and allow hands-free operation of your phone while preventing hearing damage are a must-have.
    

## Cold-weather work gear is a practical gift option

When I first started my DIY journey, my solution for working outside in the cold was to put on multiple layers of my regular clothes, which limited my mobility and made me sweat up a storm. A better idea? Some work gear designed for the cold weather:

-   [**Thermal gloves**](https://zdcs.link/QqRrZ0?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Thermal%20gloves&short_url=QqRrZ0&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") will keep hands from going numb while still offering protection.
    
-   **Base layers:** The key to staying warm without getting chilled by your own sweat while working outside lies in having the right base layers—[tops](https://zdcs.link/QPVLNm?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=tops&short_url=QPVLNm&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") and [bottoms](https://zdcs.link/zJrL0k?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=bottoms&short_url=zJrL0k&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window").
    
-   **Headgear:** Keeping the head and neck warm while working outside is the key to keeping your whole body warm, because so much heat is lost through the head. A combination [hat, scarf, and headlamp](https://zdcs.link/aXVLvp?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=hat%2C%20scarf%2C%20and%20headlamp&short_url=aXVLvp&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") solves the problem.
