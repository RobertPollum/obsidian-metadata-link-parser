[Outfitting that garage](https://lifehacker.com/home/diy-garage-upgrades-under-100) with the right gear is the difference between a useful and organized space and a chaotic black hole of junk. If you want your garage to be a place where you can get work done, where you can actually find stuff, and maybe also where you can park your car, here’s what you should have, ranging from the must-have essentials to some luxuries you could stretch for.

## What every garage needs

If you want to get the most of any garage, here’s the short list of essentials:

-   **Overhead storage.** An empty garage might seem like endless storage, but it fills up fast. If you don’t want to navigate a maze of junk every time you go in there, some [overhead storage shelving](https://zdcs.link/aN8O2B?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=overhead%20storage%20shelving&short_url=aN8O2B&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is a must.
    
-   **Wall storage.** For more readily accessible storage, some [bike hooks](https://zdcs.link/aDreqo?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=bike%20hooks&short_url=aDreqo&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") and [wall hooks](https://zdcs.link/QdpqV1?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=wall%20hooks&short_url=QdpqV1&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") for yard tools (like rakes and shovels) will keep those things easy to grab but off the floor.
    
-   **Absorbent mats.** Your car will leak oil and other fluids, and the garage is where you do a lot of messy jobs. A large-format [absorbent mat](https://zdcs.link/QOg5OM?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=absorbent%20mat&short_url=QOg5OM&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") will save you a lot of cleanup.
    
-   **Fire safety.** Every space in your home should have a [fire extinguisher](https://zdcs.link/Qp4xmw?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=fire%20extinguisher&short_url=Qp4xmw&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") within easy reach, and your garage is no exception. You might also consider keeping some [fire blankets](https://zdcs.link/z31NMP?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=fire%20blankets&short_url=z31NMP&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window"), which are often more effective for small, contained fires.
    
-   **Sports caddy.** If you’ve got a collection of balls for every possible sport, plus other implements, having [a place to dump them](https://zdcs.link/QbMvkw?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=a%20place%20to%20dump%20them&short_url=QbMvkw&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") all so they don’t roll around is a must.
    
-   **Tool storage.** Whether you go with a classic [tool box](https://zdcs.link/akwexr?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=tool%20box&short_url=akwexr&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") or a set of [magnetic strips](https://zdcs.link/9gjnpq?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=magnetic%20strips&short_url=9gjnpq&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") on the walls, don’t let your expensive, delicate tools get dirty, damp, and lost.
    
-   **Workbench.** Even if you’re not a hobbyist or much of a DIYer, having a workbench in the garage is a good idea. If space is an issue, a wall-mounted folding one [like this](https://zdcs.link/zJrG0D?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=like%20this&short_url=zJrG0D&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is an ideal solution.
    
-   **Creeper seat.** Similarly, you don’t have to be a total gearhead to appreciate a [creeper seat](https://zdcs.link/Qxx4gp?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=creeper%20seat&short_url=Qxx4gp&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window"). Working in the garage often means working down low to the floor, so unless you enjoy sitting on he cold, greasy floor for a few hours, a creeper seat is a necessity.
    

## What would be nice to have if you own a garage

You can always find an upgrade for any space, and your garage is no exception. Some useful-but-not-essential upgrades include:

-   **Rubber door bottom.** Your garage door probably isn’t a very good seal. When the weather’s hot or cold, that can make the space uncomfortable. A simple adhesive [rubber door bottom](https://zdcs.link/QWErW6?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=rubber%20door%20bottom&short_url=QWErW6&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") provides a nice seal to make the space a little nicer to be in.
    
-   **Climate control.** You don’t necessarily have to install central air or a mini-split in your garage. There are plenty of portable [heating](https://zdcs.link/Q4Dl0A?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=heating&short_url=Q4Dl0A&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") and [cooling](https://zdcs.link/a01r63?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=cooling&short_url=a01r63&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") solutions that will keep you comfy while you work without breaking the bank.
    
-   **Tire rack:** If you have a growing collection of spare tires, or you’re rotating between all-weather and winter tires regularly, having a [stable storage shelf](https://zdcs.link/aBDPvE?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=stable%20storage%20shelf&short_url=aBDPvE&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") for them is a lot better than stacking them up or having them rolling around.
    
-   **Stopper mats:** It’s a universal frustration: trying not to ram into the wall or crush a bunch of stuff every time you pull your car into the garage. If you don’t want a tennis ball hanging from a string, some simple mats [like these](https://zdcs.link/QGr3Kx?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=like%20these&short_url=QGr3Kx&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") will let you know when to hit the brakes.
    
-   **Tile flooring:** Even if you have a nice floor in your garage—maybe _especially_ if you have a nice floor in there—a [protective tile floor](https://zdcs.link/zj71Yq?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=protective%20tile%20floor&short_url=zj71Yq&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is a good idea. It’ll keep everything pristine and protected from damage (from dropped tools, for example).
    

## Best garage upgrades

Want your garage to be even nicer? No problem—here are some very useful add-ons that fall into the “luxury” category for most garages:

-   **Speakers.** These days, you don’t need a whole entertainment system in your garage—your phone and a Bluetooth speaker will do. But the speaker needs to be [waterproof and designed for work spaces](https://zdcs.link/av35B7?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=waterproof%20and%20designed%20for%20work%20spaces&short_url=av35B7&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") if you want it to survive.
    
-   **Fridge.** If you’re still nipping into the kitchen for a fresh beverage while you’re working in the garage, it’s time to upgrade to a [garage-specific fridge](https://zdcs.link/z31NAP?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=garage-specific%20fridge&short_url=z31NAP&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window").
    
-   **Retractable extension cords.** Necessary? Not really, but very useful: A [retractable extension cord](https://zdcs.link/QrA765?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=retractable%20extension%20cord&short_url=QrA765&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") keeps wires out of the way when you’re not using them and prevents them from knotting up in maddening ways. Extra bonus: Have an outlet installed in the ceiling of the garage and mount the extension cord up there, too, so you just pull it down when you need it.
    
-   **Utility sink.** Having a [place to wash up](https://zdcs.link/z6DdWY?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=place%20to%20wash%20up&short_url=z6DdWY&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") and clean dirty tools in the garage is a godsend. If the garage isn’t plumbed and you don’t want to sink that kind of cash into it, an [outdoor sink](https://zdcs.link/9yGR6n?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=outdoor%20sink&short_url=9yGR6n&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") hooked up to a garden hose will do the trick.
    
-   **Hoist.** Suckers get on ladders and lift with their muscles. Smart folks install an [overhead hoist](https://zdcs.link/QbMvGw?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=overhead%20hoist&short_url=QbMvGw&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") that can lift cargo boxes or other heavy things out of the way with ease.
    
-   **Garage door screen.** It’s nice to open the garage door while you’re working in the warm weather—unless it invites every bug in the universe to assault you. A nice [garage door screen](https://zdcs.link/QPV6NZ?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=garage%20door%20screen&short_url=QPV6NZ&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") lets you enjoy the breeze without the bugs.
    
-   **Laser guides.** If a rubber mat or a tennis ball on a string isn’t high-tech enough for your parking needs, why not install a [laser-guided system](https://zdcs.link/zn53WG?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=laser-guided%20system&short_url=zn53WG&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window")? You’ll never scrape a door or crush a garbage can again.
    
-   **Paper towel dispenser.** Necessary? Maybe not, but being able to grab a [paper towel hands-free](https://zdcs.link/zj71yM?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=paper%20towel%20hands-free&short_url=zj71yM&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") as needed just makes garage life easier.
    
-   **Wall-mounted inflator.** If you’ve got cars and bikes in your garage, installing a [wall-mounted inflator](https://zdcs.link/z7MBxn?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=wall-mounted%20inflator&short_url=z7MBxn&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") will make keeping tires properly inflated and maintained a breeze.
    
-   **Seating area.** And if you’ve got a large garage—or no car—why not create a comfortable place to sit and relax in-between projects? Some durable outdoor furniture and an outdoor rug are all you need.
