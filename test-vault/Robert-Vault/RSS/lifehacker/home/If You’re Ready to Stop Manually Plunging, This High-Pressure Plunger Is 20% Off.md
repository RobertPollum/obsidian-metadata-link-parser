High-pressure plungers work by building up air pressure inside (accomplished by pumping it up like a bicycle pump), then shooting a targeted blast of air into into the drain to blow the obstruction loose. This model produces 120 psi of pressure, which is plenty to clear most common drain and toilet clogs. It’s really easy to use, too. You just pump it up to your desired pressure (you can start at a lower psi if you’re worried about damaging your pipes), insert it into the drain, and pull the trigger.

The kit comes with different plugs for different drains, a storage bag (or a wall clip if you prefer), and, as a nice little extra, a pair of durable rubber gloves, since clearing drains and toilets can be messy work. At [20% off](https://zdcs.link/9l2rgO?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=20%25%20off&short_url=9l2rgO&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window"), this is a great deal you should jump on if you’re in one of those rare houses with drains and/or toilets in them.
