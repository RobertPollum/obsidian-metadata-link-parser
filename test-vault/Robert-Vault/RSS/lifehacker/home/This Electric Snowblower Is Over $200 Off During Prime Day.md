When I bought my house, I swore I’d never lift a shovel full of slushy snow ever again. I don’t have nearly as much sidewalk to clear, but I still love a snowblower—and recently I’ve fallen for electric blowers that run on batteries, because corded ones are a pain and I’d rather not have gas on hand all the time. So this [Greenworks 80V electric snowblower](https://zdcs.link/akwnZr?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Greenworks%2080V%20electric%20snowblower&short_url=akwnZr&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is an easy buy—especially when it’s 43% off for Prime Day.

It’s pretty lightweight, which makes pushing it around easier than some of those gas-powered behemoths, and it has a solid half-hour runtime if you store the batteries indoors (and obviously, you can extend that by having multiple batteries). It’s powerful enough to chew through heavy snow and it throws it a good distance, so you’ll be surprised by how much snow you can clear in just one charge. The batteries charge up in about a half hour, too.
