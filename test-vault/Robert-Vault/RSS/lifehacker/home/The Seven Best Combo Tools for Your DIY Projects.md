There are two universal truths about tools: They’re [expensive](https://lifehacker.com/five-hand-tools-its-ok-to-buy-cheap-1850651529), and they take up a lot of space. If you’re trying to maintain a home or perform some DIY work in a small space or on a small budget, stacking up all the tools you might need to get the job done can [strain your wallet](https://lifehacker.com/home/best-places-buy-used-refurbished-tools) and simultaneously pose a storage challenge.

The solution is actually pretty easy: Buy tools that combine more than one function into a single item. While having the specific tool you need for every job will always be your best choice, if you lack space and/or cash, combination tools like these give you all the capability you need without eating up all your storage space—or draining your bank account.

You might already have a multitool, but that’s nothing compared to this [useful combination tool](https://zdcs.link/aM8ky6?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=useful%20combination%20tool&short_url=aM8ky6&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window"). You’re not going to be doing major renovation projects with this, but if you need a complete toolbox for everyday DIY and have no space or budget for real tools, this will get just about every job done.

## This five-in-one drill packs a lot of power into a small unit

This [five-in-one drill from Dewalt](https://zdcs.link/QqRlMD?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=five-in-one%20drill%20from%20Dewalt&short_url=QqRlMD&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") is a terrific combination tool for two reasons: It’s multifunctional, and it combines a small size with a lot of power so it can be used in just about any situation. You can bore holes and drive fasteners while easily changing the angle and position of your bit—the right-angle and offset attachments plus its small size means you don’t need any other drill to get your jobs done.

## This eight-in-one hand saw has swappable blades

Having the right saw for the job is essential—trying to use a wood saw when a hacksaw would be a better choice is never going to work. This [eight-in-one hand saw](https://zdcs.link/zEJZ17?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=eight-in-one%20hand%20saw&short_url=zEJZ17&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Fhome "open in a new window") offers swappable blades for wood, drywall, metal, and plastic, allowing you to cut a wide range of materials without having to collect and swap out multiple saws. It also features a quick-change mechanism, so if you’re working with multiple materials, you can quickly switch between them.

## This adjustable wrench is much more

Instead of four or five tools taking up space, this one small piece will do it all for you.
