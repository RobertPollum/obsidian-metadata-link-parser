Dates can already be stressful, but throw in the curveball of their costs, and, well, they can feel more anxiety-inducing than they’re worth. It’s normal to want to “wow” your date with a solid first impression, but the good news is that they don’t need be super expensive. You can surprise your date with something unique and budget-friendly, and that isn’t just going for a walk in the park.  

## Attend a Pride event at your local museum

You can celebrate Pride and learn about contributions from the LGBTQIA+ community at your local museum. Many museums host a series of events, workshops, discussions, and performances throughout the month of June and beyond, including [the Met in New York](https://www.metmuseum.org/worldpride "open in a new window") ($30 admission fee), the [Minneapolis Institute of Art](https://new.artsmia.org/pride-at-mia/ "open in a new window") (free admission), and the [WNDR Museums](https://wndrmuseum.com/ "open in a new window") in both Chicago and Seattle ($38).  

## Ping Pod is bringing ping pong back

Ping pong is making a comeback. [Ping Pod](https://www.timeout.com/newyork/news/24/7-ping-pong-pods-are-coming-to-brooklyn-and-manhattan-this-year-012423 "open in a new window") are 24/7 ping pong pods that are popping across New York, New Jersey, and Philadelphia, with plans for more locations in Miami and Boston. Pricing ranges from $15-$50/session depending on demand.  

## Candlelight Concerts

Everyone knows a little candlelight can add a lot of romance. Tack that ambiance onto a night filled with heart-swelled music, and you’ve got yourself a fancy date for less than $50. Appearing in over 100 cities, [Candlelight Concerts](https://candlelightexperience.com/ "open in a new window") are unique candlelit performances featuring a wide variety of themes and genres: classical, jazz, pop, movie soundtracks, ballet, among others. Tickets vary but most fall within the $30-$50 range.  

## The Friends Experience

If you’re both fans of the old network sitcom _Friends_, you can check out the [Friends Experience](https://www.friendstheexperience.com/ "open in a new window"), which basically transports you to the famous ‘90s sitcom with sets, props, costumes, and tons of photo ops. The unique experience travels throughout the country (and the world) and is now in New York and Brussels, and will soon open in Detroit, Birmingham, and Long Beach. Tickets start at $49.50.  

## Take a ghost walking tour

If your date loves true crime podcasts and creepy Netflix docuseries, inviting them for a ghost walking tour in your city might be up their alley (and an excuse to hold hands). A number of ghost walking tours will take you to various buildings where paranormal activity has been reported, while also sharing bits of history along the way. Some notable tours include the [Greenwich Village Ghost Tour](https://topdogtours.com/tours/new-york-city/ghosts-of-greenwich-village/ "open in a new window") ($39), the [Chicago Gangster and Ghosts Tour](https://gangstersandghosts.com/ "open in a new window") ($35) and [the Dallas Terrors Ghost Tour](https://dallasterrors.com/ "open in a new window") ($25).  

## Your local science center

Love is all about chemistry, so it makes sense to explore and learn more about it with your new date, along with other science-y things at your local science center. From unique events to after-hours socials, lectures, discussions, community science projects, and pub nights, there are a number of ways to connect with your date while learning something new. For example, try [Fairy Tales After Dark](https://secure.scienceworksmuseum.org/np/clients/scienceworksmuseum/event.jsp?forwardedFromSecureDomain=1&event=43648 "open in a new window") at the [ScienceWorks Hands-On Museum](https://scienceworksmuseum.org/ "open in a new window") in Ashland, Oregon ($20) or the [Social Science events](https://nvdm.org/event/social-science-cowboys-and-aliens/ "open in a new window") ($30) at the [Terry Lee Wells Nevada Discovery Museum](https://sciencenearme.org/find?sort=closest&text=%22Terry%20Lee%20Wells%20Nevada%20Discovery%20Museum%22&utm_campaign=datenight&utm_medium=partnersite&utm_source=Disco&utm_content=reno1 "open in a new window") in Reno, Nevada.  

## Your local botanical garden

The sights and sounds of nature are practically the original aphrodisiacs. Many botanical gardens offer a slew of events, from gardening workshops to oral history tours to meditation hours that could add just the right dose of uniqueness to your date. This is an especially great idea if they’re a budding green thumb. You might consider the [Power of Trees Exhibit](https://www.bbg.org/poweroftrees "open in a new window") at the Brooklyn Botanical Garden ($18), the [Trolls Save the Humans Exhibit](https://atlantabg.org/trolls/ "open in a new window") at the Atlanta Botanical Garden ($26.95), or experience [Chiluly in the Garden](https://www.missouribotanicalgarden.org/events-classes/special-exhibitions/chihuly-in-the-garden-2023 "open in a new window") at the Missouri Botanical Garden ($20-$25), which at night features cocktails and live music.
