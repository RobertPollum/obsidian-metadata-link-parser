The weather might not be as warm once the leaves start changing, but that doesn’t mean your love life needs to cool down. There are many ways to enjoy the change of the season without going full-on PSL-drinking, plaid-wearing basic coupledom. Planning a fall date doesn’t have to hurt your wallet, nor does it have to be cringe-y and cliché. Below are some unique fall dating ideas to heat up your relationship this season.  

## Take a class together

School kicks off for many in the fall, so if receiving a bouquet of newly sharpened pencils is your aphrodisiac of choice, then you might consider taking a class together with a partner. Whether it’s a one-off session—like a cooking class on a Friday night—or a weekly class, going back to school together could provide some major bonding time.

## Go wardrobe shopping

There’s a reason why Vogue’s September issue is iconic—fall is _the_ season to change up your wardrobe and clean out your closet as you get ready for the colder days ahead. Going shopping together as you each restock your wardrobe with knits and layers can be a terrific bonding experience. You can try on clothing in front of each other and suggest input, get some ideas, and get to know each other better through your sense of style.

## Visit a bookstore

If you’re both bookworms, stocking up on books for chilly fall days is a must. Plus, what’s cozier and more romantic than a bookstore in the fall? If you can, try to find an independent bookstore in your neighborhood—preferably one with a cafe—and get lost in conversation over your latest book picks as you linger at a table for the afternoon.  

## Volunteer at a local soup kitchen or food bank  

With more and more people relying on food banks and other food charities to make ends meet, now is a great time to volunteer your time at a local organization as they prepare for the fall and winter season. Not only does volunteering together double your impact, it’s a wonderful way to strengthen your bond by supporting a cause you’re both passionate about. It also helps to identify and intensify the core values you’re developing together as a couple.

## Organize a flag football date

If you’re both athletic and like to play football, organize a group flag football date with you and your friends at your local park. A little healthy competition is always fun, and working out together is known to add some much-needed benefits like boosting endorphins, which helps to elevate your mood—and increase your sex drive.

## Go for a drive in the country

Fall is the most colorful season, so hit the road to enjoy the scenery and foliage. Make it a day trip by taking a thermos of coffee or hot chocolate, uploading your favorite songs on a playlist, and hitting up gas stations for road trip junk food. Extend your trip by booking a cottage in the mountains. Traveling is a great way to improve your connection; it allows you to enjoy a shared experience while building memories together.
