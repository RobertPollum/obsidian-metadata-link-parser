Whether your idea of a perfect vacation is sipping high-end cocktails at a five-star resort in Madrid or choking down a bowl of ayahuasca in a cave in Panama, there's a travel influencer out there for you.

The 14 travel influencers below are widely considered the best in the world. They range from van-lifers to international jet-setters, but each captures the spirit of exploration and adventure of travel in their own way.

## Johnny Jet

I'm starting this list with Johnny Jet, because he's the O.G. travel influencer—Jet's been about that life since 1995, before many travel influencers were even born. Jet, real name John Discala, parlayed his first-one-in status into a travel empire. He's appeared on every major network, hosted his own show, _Hot Spots,_ on the Travel Channel, and his [website](https://johnnyjet.com/ "open in a new window") is updated constantly with everything from general news about travel, to credit card recommendation, to money-saving tips.

## Fun for Louis

Louis Cole, aka "[Fun for Louis,](https://www.youtube.com/@Louis "open in a new window")" has attracted over two million followers for his laid-back travelog YouTube videos where he skateboards, snowboards, and tramps all over the world. Cole has an engaging personal style that works perfectly with his stunt-style videos, where he does things like crossing the English Channel on an electric surfboard and riding a [solar-powered scooter from the UK to Italy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nxnLryAItsk&t=355s "open in a new window").

## Trek Trendy

Trek Trendy has over a million YouTube subscribers on his [travel channel](https://www.youtube.com/@Trektrendy "open in a new window"). While he focuses on high-end, ultra-luxury travel like [a trip on the Orient Express](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gSxqZC6mApw "open in a new window") (complete with caviar and tuxedos) he's so down-to-earth and friendly, it never feels like he's rubbing it in your face.

## Grace and Matt Grooms

![Grave and Matt Grooms](https://lifehacker.com/imagery/articles/01J2D27213QJGJXN2BQ2V9J4Y5/images-2.fill.size_2000x1343.v1720572938.png)

Credit: Grace and Matt Grooms/Instagram

Grace and Matt, aka The Golden Road, are the opposite end of the travel spectrum from Trek Trendy. Instead of paying six-figures for a ticket on the Orient Express, this traveling couple tour the U.S. with their dogs and posting the result on their Instagram. They take [very long walks](https://www.instagram.com/reel/Cx3T-mwuKw4/?hl=en "open in a new window"). They [renovate their school bus](https://www.instagram.com/reel/C8IhsnovEIv/?hl=en "open in a new window"). Sometimes [they camp.](https://www.instagram.com/p/CzW3saeOGA1/?hl=en "open in a new window") Sometimes [they don’t](https://www.instagram.com/reel/CvXxb47ggJG/?hl=en "open in a new window"). Throughout, they embark on [attainable travel adventures](https://www.instagram.com/reel/CzMCfA7O7Zc/?hl=en "open in a new window") that are presented in a friendly style.  

## Alex Strohl

![Alex Strohl's Instagram](https://lifehacker.com/imagery/articles/01J2D27213QJGJXN2BQ2V9J4Y5/images-3.fill.size_2000x1328.v1720572938.png)

Credit: Alex Strohl/Instagram

French photographer Alex Strohl's [travel instagram](https://www.instagram.com/alexstrohl/ "open in a new window") regularly features breathtakingly beautiful images, from [Hawaii](https://www.instagram.com/p/C6H7mf8vb_0/?img_index=1 "open in a new window"), to [Colorado](https://www.instagram.com/p/C2-wq_-pV3K/?img_index=1 "open in a new window"), to the [Pyrénées](https://www.instagram.com/p/Cwf-AJjILHf/?img_index=1 "open in a new window") and everywhere else. If you're into the pure aesthetic wonderment of travel, point your browser to Strohl's Insta—it's way better than your great-aunt's Facebook pics from that time she went to Rome.

## The Blonde Abroad

![Kiki aka The Blonde Abroad](https://lifehacker.com/imagery/articles/01J2D27213QJGJXN2BQ2V9J4Y5/images-4.fill.size_2000x836.v1720572938.jpg)

Credit: Kiki

Kiki, aka [The Blonde Abroad](https://www.theblondeabroad.com/ "open in a new window"), started her popular travel blog in 2011, and she's been everywhere since, from [Guatemala](https://www.theblondeabroad.com/where-to-find-the-best-food-in-antigua/ "open in a new window") to [Iceland](https://www.theblondeabroad.com/ice-climbing-iceland/ "open in a new window"). Kiki's goal is to "help women find the confidence to travel the world," and her site is packed with [no-nonsense tips](https://www.theblondeabroad.com/10-safest-destinations-solo-female-travelers/ "open in a new window") for [solo traveling](https://www.theblondeabroad.com/solo-travel/ "open in a new window") along with inspiring photography and genial, conversational writing.

## Noel Phillips

Most travel influencers create content about their destinations, but [Noel Phillips](https://www.youtube.com/@noelphilips "open in a new window")’ passion is the _airplanes_. He travels on obscure, little known, often [dangerous](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NOGHLNHc51s "open in a new window") airlines (and sometimes [trains](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DXQZTMJrcoE "open in a new window")) and invites you to sit beside him for the ride. Dude has an infectious passion for aviation, and watching him do things like fly a transatlantic flight on [Algeria’s airline](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YmhWoNm_fCA "open in a new window") will really make you appreciate the luxury and opulence of Spirit Air. 

## Camping with Steve

Whether he's [camping in a parking garage](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBnlPd__Vg0 "open in a new window"), [posting up on an abandoned highway](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S0EykVi_2e0 "open in a new window"), or testing out an [air-conditioned tent](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v1aGEV2Uggs "open in a new window"), YouTuber Steve Wallis lives his motto: Always be camping. He mainly engages in "stealth camping," i.e. setting up shop in places he's not allowed to, like a [police station](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yMIA6zdUVCA "open in a new window"), a [donation bin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=frXdtNI3Ylg "open in a new window"), and a [roundabout](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OPDvLaXuSHQ "open in a new window"). The "will he get caught?" aspect of his videos make them uniquely suspenseful.

## Angelica Blick

![Angelica Blick](https://lifehacker.com/imagery/articles/01J2D27213QJGJXN2BQ2V9J4Y5/images-1.fill.size_2000x1336.v1720572938.jpg)

Credit: Angelica Blick/Instagram

My idea of traveling is very different from Angelica Blick’s. She’s about [high-end, luxury touring](https://www.instagram.com/reel/C6otbCrIVQQ/?hl=en "open in a new window") and [looking fabulous](https://www.instagram.com/reel/C4bOtRMoLWu/?hl=en "open in a new window") when you’re doing it. We probably wouldn’t like hanging out, but I love [her Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/angelicablick/?hl=en "open in a new window") anyway. Her carefully composed and styled pictures and videos are a look into another world that’s more [stylish and dreamlike](https://www.instagram.com/reel/C2-XCSEINVF/?hl=en "open in a new window") than my own. 

## Kuga's Travel

Kuga's Travel was created by a Japanese YouTuber who visits interesting places and mainly films the train cars and airplane cabins that take them there, with no voice over, only written captions. So if you want to take a relaxing point-of-view ride on [Australia's most expensive train](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rMbX1dqNU_c "open in a new window"), a [Chicago L train](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sbq8uf25KWc "open in a new window"), or a [British hovercraft](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSkO0Ol_KPw "open in a new window"), and you don't want any yammering, check out Kuga's Travel.

## Urbex Hill

Maybe "urban exploring" doesn't count as "travel," but I'm putting [Urbex Hill](https://www.youtube.com/c/URBEXHILL "open in a new window") here anyway; call the internet-list police if you must. Hill's passion is taking deep dives into [decrepit](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9k5KpjkP2GU "open in a new window"), [abandoned](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Aj-mF6qY5pM "open in a new window"), and [scary-ass places](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HDbEtwbvwuk "open in a new window") in and around Cleveland. While his videos sometimes get into the [supernatural](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wrYAKTysTx4 "open in a new window"), ghosts are the last things I'd be afraid of in [some of his locations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=inpillaYLQc "open in a new window").

## Lost Leblanc

I'm not a huge fan of [YouTuber Lost Leblanc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P3RWXEvaiwo "open in a new window"), but his slickly produced videos like [BILLIONAIRE for a week - Most EXPENSIVE of DUBAI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6sAgglqMSBA "open in a new window") and [How YOU can Travel Full Time & Make Money on Social Media](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u0hrhZoL9Cg "open in a new window") represent a very popular subset of trendy travel influencer. I find his style smarmy and cheesy, but he has more than two million followers and gets paid to do luxury travel, so what do I know?

## Indigo Traveller

There's a subset of first world travel YouTubers whose bag is touring locations both the U.S. State Department and common sense strongly suggest you avoid. These kinds of accounts raise troubling ethical questions too complex to get into here, but if you can deal with it, the content they produce is fascinating. YouTuber [Indigo Traveller](https://www.youtube.com/@IndigoTraveller "open in a new window") is a New Zealander who seeks out [active war zones](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HGPaoKPck4g "open in a new window"), the most ["dangerous" neighborhoods](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V2kolF4yo-Q "open in a new window"), and other [places he shouldn't be](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BaXUZcVHQEE "open in a new window"), and seems to try to take a non-exploitative, empathetic view of the people there.

## The Carpetbagger

The Carpetbagger is a traveler [after my own heart.](https://lifehacker.com/11-deeply-strange-u-s-tourist-spots-1849870496) A self-described "genuine yankee moron," he visits every tourist trap and unusual attraction to bring back fun, unapologetically nerdy vlogs of [huge balls of twin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=43KHBU_kAeg "open in a new window")e, [walk-in elephants](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u8ywgRdi98U "open in a new window"), and all manner of other unusual attractions.
