_Welcome to "[Best Summer Ever,](https://lifehacker.com/series/best-summer-ever)__" your guide to getting the most out of the sunny season. Whether your idea of a perfect summer is embarking on epic adventures or blissfully doing as little as possible, we've got you covered. Because the best summer doesn't just happen—you have to_ make _it happen._

My summer cannot be "best" unless I spend a _lot_ of time on beaches. I crave the time-has-stopped feeling of spending all day in a place where it's _impossible_ to do anything useful. A lifetime of hanging out on sand near water has taught me that it's easier to achieve "beach zen" if you plan ahead and bring the right gear—the difference between a great day at the beach and sandy headache comes down to what you pack. Below is everything you need for the best beach day ever, from towels and shades to underwater scooters.

## The best beach basics

I'm from a beach town, so I used to roll up to the sand with just a ratty towel—even a pair of flip-flops was extra baggage. There's something to be said for minimalist beach-bum style, but these days, I like things to be more comfortable. I'm usually bringing the family too, and that requires _stuff_, so there are a few must-haves I always bring.

### Towels and beach mats

Instead of showing up with a bleached out, threadbare towel from 2004, consider a microfiber towel. They're more absorbent than regular towels, they dry faster, they're lighter, and they're super cheap. Less than $10 for a [a generous ‎72 x 36 microfiber beach towel with your choice of bold beachy designs.](https://zdcs.link/xPLLb?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=a%20generous%20%E2%80%8E72%20x%2036%20microfiber%20beach%20towel%20with%20your%20choice%20of%20bold%20beachy%20designs.&short_url=xPLLb&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window")

This [picnic blanket](https://zdcs.link/lVrVd?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=picnic%20blanket&short_url=lVrVd&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") with an attached strap is a higher-end option. It's easy to carry, waterproof, and versatile.

If you're staking out a lot of ground, nylon beach mats are a great option. I don't love how they feel lying on them, but you can get a [10' by 9' beach mat](https://zdcs.link/Od448?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=10%27%20by%209%27%20beach%20mat&short_url=Od448&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") that packs up small and weighs little, then put your (generously sun-blocked) toddler in the middle of it and watch them crawl around.

### Sun shades

I like shade, but I hate beach umbrellas. They're a bear to set up, and a sudden gust of wind can cause them to take flight, turning them into dangerous projectiles. There are better options.

I really like the [Shibumi Shade](https://zdcs.link/oeNGm?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Shibumi%20Shade&short_url=oeNGm&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window"). I was a little skeptical of whether this ultralight piece of fabric would float on the breeze as advertised, but I got one, and it works, no cap. If there's no breeze on your beach (lucky), you can pick up [an accessory that makes wind](https://zdcs.link/z3GyRV?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=an%20accessory%20that%20makes%20wind&short_url=z3GyRV&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") for you.

### Personal sun protection

**Sun hat**: Big straw hats like [this one from Quiksilver](https://zdcs.link/0Ppm5?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=this%20one%20from%20Quiksilver&short_url=0Ppm5&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") have been a staple among Southern California surfers since the 1960s and absolutely can't be improved upon. Generous sun protection and a cool, locals only, look.

**Sunglasses**: I love my Ray-Ban Meta sunglasses, even after wearing them [regularly for over six months](https://lifehacker.com/tech/what-i-learned-after-six-months-of-wearing-ray-ban-meta-smart-glasses). They hover around $300, but they do a _lot_, including letting you [capture video on the fly that looks like this](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GXILj0I4toI "open in a new window").

**Sunscreen**: I always use [Banana Boat sunscreen](https://zdcs.link/vdRd0?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Banana%20Boat%20sunscreen&short_url=vdRd0&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") because it's what they tend to have at the drug store, and my face has never burned off. But I'm not refined. If you want a sunscreen that's actually _good_ for your face, [Marlowe's 128](https://zdcs.link/QdbZbp?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Marlowe%27s%20128&short_url=QdbZbp&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") is niiiice.

These features won't come in handy very often, but there may come a time when you need to start an instant beach party, and you'll be _ready_.

If you're only packing a few things and you don't need loudspeaker, over-the-shoulder carried coolers like this [Polarbox 1 Ice Chest](https://zdcs.link/2e3NM?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Polarbox%201%20Ice%20Chest&short_url=2e3NM&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") have a retro appeal.

## What to bring for fun in the water

With the basics settled, let's talk about fun things you can _do_ at the beach, starting with the thing that differentiates a beach from a pile of dirt: the water.

I don't know whether this [Waydoo Flyer One](https://zdcs.link/QxAmXD?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Waydoo%20Flyer%20One&short_url=QxAmXD&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") is a great example of an electric hydrofoil, but please buy me one and I'll let you know.

## What to bring for fun in the sand

I might say doing nothing on the sand is the personal goal, but you don't want _absolutely_ nothing to do. So pack these "almost nothing" things to do:

### An e-reader

I used to always bring a book to the beach. Now I pack a [Kindle Paperwhite](https://zdcs.link/QOb3bY?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Kindle%20Paperwhite&short_url=QOb3bY&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window"). Kindles are better than books in every way. They can stay under the lake for an hour and still work (try that with a copy of _War and Peace_). Unlike antique _paper_ books, Kindles are Bluetooth-compatible for when you decide you'd rather listen to a podcast anyway. (The Kindle will be OK if you drop it in fresh water, but salt and sand is a different story, so get a [screen protector](https://zdcs.link/71ppP?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=screen%20protector&short_url=71ppP&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window").)

### A camera

It's not a day at the beach if you don't have a record of it, right? So bring a camera (unless there's one in your sunglasses) I love my [GoPro.](https://zdcs.link/a03b3p?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=GoPro.&short_url=a03b3p&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") This small camera is rugged as hell, fully waterproof, mounts onto literally anything, and has almost no learning curve. Check out this [glowing GoPro Hero 13 review](https://lifehacker.com/tech/gopro-hero-13-black-review) for a lot more detail.

### A deck of cards

Little is more beach-zen than breaking out the cards and playing spades on the beach. I like these [waterproof playing cards from Hoyle](https://zdcs.link/5MLwV?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=waterproof%20playing%20cards%20from%20Hoyle&short_url=5MLwV&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window"). They’re practically indestructible, so you can play Bridge while whitewater rafting.

### A guitar

Bringing a guitar to the beach is like wearing sunglasses at night. Most people shouldn't do it, but some people should _always_ do it. "The beach will damage my delicate guitar!" you might be saying. Yeah, that's why you need a carbon fiber guitar, like this [Klos Deluxe](https://zdcs.link/LbdY0?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Klos%20Deluxe&short_url=LbdY0&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") acoustic/electric. They can plug right into your cooler, and they're practically indestructible—a feature that will come in handy if you're playing "Wonderwall" through a cooler.

### A Bluetooth speaker

If you like a little music at the beach, but a cooler-based sound system is a bit much for your personal style, pick up one of these beach-ready [SoundLink Flex speakers](https://zdcs.link/GdygB?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=SoundLink%20Flex%20speakers&short_url=GdygB&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") from Bose. They’re IP67 waterproof, so they can be fully immersed in water with no ill effects, and they're designed to resist outdoor threats like dust, sand, and UV light.

### An extra charger

You will be the hero of the beach if you can whip out a charger when someone's phone dies. I like this [Poseidon Nano Charger.](https://zdcs.link/1vg1q?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Poseidon%20Nano%20Charger.&short_url=1vg1q&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") It's small, but packs eight hours of go-juice. Plus, it’s water-resistant, and it holds a charge for up to two years. 

Speaking of phones, they overheat at the beach—but not if you pack them in a [thermal phone case.](https://zdcs.link/Bd5gN?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=thermal%20phone%20case.&short_url=Bd5gN&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") Not only do they reflect sunlight and heat in the summer, they protect your phone from extreme cold in the winter too. 

### A personal air conditioner

I have heard rumors that there are people who don't _like_ the beach, who don't see the fun of doing nothing all day in the hot sun. Those people need to hang a [personal air conditioners](https://zdcs.link/xP0pD?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=personal%20air%20conditioners&short_url=xP0pD&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftravel "open in a new window") around their necks.
