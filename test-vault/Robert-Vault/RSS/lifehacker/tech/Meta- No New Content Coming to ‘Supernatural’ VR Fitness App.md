Users of [_Supernatural_](https://zdcs.link/QW6Xnn?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Supernatural&short_url=QW6Xnn&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftech "open in a new window") got an unpleasant surprise this week: Meta has pulled the plug on its flagship virtual reality fitness app. Citing "organizational changes," Meta says it will no longer release new content or update features for _Supernatural_.

The app is not shutting down completely however. Subscribers can still access _Supernatural's_ existing library of _Beat Saber_\-workouts, and Meta says it will maintain the platform and Facebook page, but no new workouts, features, or other content is planned.

Both users and critics have nearly universally praised _Supernatural_—[CNet scored it 9 out of 10](https://www.cnet.com/tech/computing/supernatural-review-my-go-to-for-vr-fitness-now/ "open in a new window"), it won both [Fast Company's Best App award](https://www.fastcompany.com/90547929/apps-and-games-innovation-by-design-2020 "open in a new window") in 2020 and [a Webby in 2023](https://winners.webbyawards.com/winners/ai-immersive-games/immersive-features/best-vr-headset-experience?years=2 "open in a new window"), and boasted celebrity tie-ins with Jane Fonda and Bon Jovi. Meta doesn't publish subscriber numbers for _Supernatural_, but there are over 110,000 members of [_Supernatural'_s Facebook community](https://www.facebook.com/groups/2680672221985841/ "open in a new window"). Not enough, apparently, to warrant keeping the app going.

In 2021, Meta spent an estimated [$400 million to purchase Within,](https://www.roadtovr.com/report-meta-wins-ftc-acquisition-within-vr-app/ "open in a new window") _Supernatural's_ developer, even battling the FTC to make the deal, and the app was a heavily promoted part of the company's overall "Metaverse" strategy.

## Meta shifts from VR to AI wearables

The shuttering of _Supernatural_ is part of a larger shift at Meta. This week, the company laid off 1,500 people—about 10% of the staff—from Reality Labs, Meta's hardware and virtual reality division. “We said last month that we were shifting some of our investment from Metaverse toward Wearables. This is part of that effort,” a Meta spokesman told [_The Wall Street Journal_](https://www.wsj.com/tech/meta-layoffs-reality-labs-2026-347008b0 "open in a new window").

Along with cuts at _Supernatural_, Meta is closing three studios behind some of the most prominent, high-end VR games: Armature, who brought [_Resident Evil 4_](https://zdcs.link/ae8MW6?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Resident%20Evil%204&short_url=ae8MW6&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftech "open in a new window") to VR, Sanzaru, the studio behind [_Asgard’s Wrath_](https://zdcs.link/9yPq12?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Asgard%E2%80%99s%20Wrath&short_url=9yPq12&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftech "open in a new window"), and Twisted Pixel, creators of [_Deadpool VR_](https://zdcs.link/QPxYlO?pageview_type=RSS&template=content&module=content_body&element=offer&item=text-link&element_label=Deadpool%20VR&short_url=QPxYlO&u=https%3A%2F%2Flifehacker.com%2Ffeed%2Frss%2Ftech "open in a new window").
