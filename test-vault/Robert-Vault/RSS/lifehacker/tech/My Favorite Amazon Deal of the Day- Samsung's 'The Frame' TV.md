Samsung’s The Frame smart TVs have come a long way since their 2017 debut. Launched to appeal to the crowd that wants a big TV but doesn’t want that TV to dominate their decor, its main feature is an anti-reflective matte display that gives it a “framed artwork” vibe—and with 4K picture quality. As the name implies, the allure of this TV is that it can double as a digital art frame thanks to its anti-glare matte display and customizable frame.

If you're skeptical a TV can really look like art, my brother fooled me into thinking his 2022 model was an actual painting. The matte screen does a good job of making the TV look unlike a TV while not impacting the viewing experience (it's actually great for bright rooms that might otherwise result in a lot of glare on the screen). That version received only an ["average" review from PCMag](https://www.pcmag.com/reviews/samsung-43-inch-class-the-frame-qled-tv "open in a new window"), mainly due to poor contrast and the price. The [2024 version](https://www.zdnet.com/home-and-office/home-entertainment/samsung-frame-tv-2024-vs-2023-comparing-the-key-differences/ "open in a new window") offered notable improvements, including a 120Hz refresh rate and 4K resolution with a Quantum 4K processor. The latest 2025 [Pro version](https://www.pcmag.com/reviews/samsung-65-inch-the-frame-pro "open in a new window") also adds wireless connectivity, which makes it easier to hang on the wall with no cable clutter.

You can buy frames separately to suit your taste—[this guy on TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@willgiffard/video/7397457247044930848 "open in a new window") [even kitted his out with a Harry Potter theme](https://www.tiktok.com/@willgiffard/video/7397457247044930848 "open in a new window").
