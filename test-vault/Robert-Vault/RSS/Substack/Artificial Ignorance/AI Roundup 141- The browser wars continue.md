### The browser wars continue

OpenAI launched [ChatGPT Atlas](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-atlas), the latest AI-powered web browser with “agent mode” that can autonomously navigate websites and complete multi-step tasks on users’ behalf.

**The big picture:**

-   Atlas builds on OpenAI’s previous agentic AI attempts like Operator and ChatGPT Agent, featuring personalized memory, a persistent ChatGPT companion in split-screen mode, and “cursor chat” for in-line text editing. But [early testing reveals](https://arstechnica.com/features/2025/10/we-let-openais-agent-mode-surf-the-web-for-us-heres-what-happened/) a slow, clunky experience, with significant limitations on session length.
    
-   The [security implications](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Oct/21/introducing-chatgpt-atlas) are substantial, as even [OpenAI’s CISO](https://x.com/cryps1s/status/1981037851279278414) acknowledged: prompt injection attacks remain an “unsolved frontier security problem” that could allow malicious websites to hijack the agent and leak credentials.
    
-   By launching a browser, OpenAI is [directly competing](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/803475/openais-ai-powered-browser-chatgpt-atlas-google-chrome-competition-agent) with Google, Microsoft, and Apple on their home turf - not to mention Perplexity’s Comet and Opera’s Neon. However, whether users want an AI intermediary for everyday web tasks - and whether they’ll trust it with sensitive actions - remains an open question.

### Shrinking Superintelligence

Meta is cutting [roughly 600 jobs](https://www.axios.com/2025/10/22/meta-superintelligence-tbd-ai-reorg) from its AI Superintelligence Lab (out of several thousand), as Mark Zuckerberg works to streamline AI operations that have become “overly bureaucratic.”

**Between the lines:**

-   This isn’t a cost-cutting measure - Meta is betting that starting fresh with new leadership and a smaller, “more load-bearing” team will outperform years of accumulated institutional knowledge, including established teams from FAIR, product AI, and infrastructure.
    
-   The restructuring comes amid reports that top AI researchers across Silicon Valley are working [80-100 hour workweeks](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/ai-race-tech-workers-schedule-1ea9a116) in a wartime-like effort to compress decades of scientific progress into years - turning the small cluster of elite AI researchers into millionaires who have no time to spend their fortunes.
    
-   Meta’s reorg reveals anxiety about falling behind, especially considering the layoffs included over 100 employees in its [risk review organization](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/23/technology/meta-layoffs-user-privacy.html) - the team responsible for ensuring compliance with FTC privacy requirements.

-   Anthropic and Google announce their [cloud partnership](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/23/anthropic-google-cloud-deal-tpu.html) worth tens of billions of dollars, giving Anthropic access to 1M TPUs and 1GW of capacity in 2026.
    
-   Microsoft unveils [Mico](https://www.theverge.com/news/804106/microsoft-mico-copilot-ai-assistant-clippy), a new character for Copilot’s voice mode that responds with real-time expressions, and [Copilot Groups](https://www.theverge.com/news/804122/microsoft-copilot-real-talk-mode-group-chats-features), enabling up to 32 people to collaborate in a session.
    
-   Amazon is testing [AR glasses](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/amazon-unveils-ai-powered-augmented-reality-glasses-for-delivery-drivers/) for delivery drivers, using AI and computer vision to help them scan packages, follow walking directions, and get proof of delivery.
    
-   YouTube launches its [likeness detection tech](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/21/youtubes-likeness-detection-technology-has-officially-launched/), letting eligible creators in its Partner Program request the removal of AI-generated content with their likeness.
    
-   Meta plans to add new [Instagram teen safety tools](https://www.theverge.com/news/801505/meta-ai-chatbot-parental-controls-instagram) in 2026 that let parents block teens from chatting with AI characters and get “insights” from teens’ chats.
    
-   And WhatsApp updates its Business API terms to [ban general-purpose chatbots](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/18/whatssapp-changes-its-terms-to-bar-general-purpose-chatbots-from-its-platform/) starting January 15, 2026, affecting WhatsApp assistants of OpenAI, Perplexity, and others.
    

**Elsewhere in AI anxiety:**

-   An EBU/BBC study found that 45% of responses from top AI assistants [misrepresented news content](https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/ai-assistants-make-widespread-errors-about-news-new-research-shows-2025-10-21/) with at least one significant issue, and 31% showed serious sourcing problems.

### DeepSeek diplomacy

While American AI companies compete for lucrative Western markets, [DeepSeek and Huawei](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-10-22/china-s-deepseek-pushes-into-africa-making-ai-accessible-to-millions) have been expanding into Africa with affordable, open-source AI models to build long-term influence across the continent.

**Why it matters:**

-   When it comes to African businesses, Chinese open-source models are available at a fraction of the price of Western competitors: DeepSeek charges $1.10 per million output tokens compared to OpenAI’s $15.
    
-   This mirrors China’s Belt and Road Initiative: it’s not about immediate profit, but about securing future customers, soft power, and vast data troves while African nations lack alternatives.
    
-   And it’s not just APIs: ByteDance’s chatbot Cici is quietly gaining traction in the UK, Mexico, and Southeast Asia. But US companies won’t give up emerging markets without a fight: [ChatGPT Go](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/11989085-what-is-chatgpt-go), OpenAI’s sub-$5 subscription for India and Southeast Asia, is also [coming to Africa](https://techcabal.com/2025/10/23/openai-brings-chatgpts-cheapest-plan-to-nigeria-kenya-south-africa/).
    

**Elsewhere in frontier models:**

-   DeepSeek released [DeepSeek-OCR](https://the-decoder.com/deepseeks-ocr-system-compresses-image-based-text-so-ai-can-handle-much-longer-documents/), a vision language model designed for efficient vision-text compression that enables longer contexts with less compute.
    
-   Anthropic announced [Claude Life Sciences](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/20/anthropic-claude-life-sciences-research-ai.html), a new feature that integrates Claude AI models with lab tools like Benchling to boost research efficiency.
    
-   Google plans to release [Gemini 3](https://sources.news/p/google-readies-gemini-3-perplexity) in December, which is expected to be more performant across the board, especially in coding and multimodal generation.
    
-   Anthropic’s Claude [memory feature](https://www.zdnet.com/article/one-of-chatgpts-most-helpful-features-is-coming-to-claude-heres-who-gets-it/) is rolling out to Pro and Max subscribers after initially being available for Team and Enterprise users.
    
-   And in an experiment, GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet 4.5, and DeepSeek-V3.2-Exp expressed [secular, Western liberal values](https://www.theargumentmag.com/p/do-ais-think-differently-in-different) regardless of the language of the questions.
    

**Elsewhere in AI geopolitics:**

-   Dario Amodei addresses [“inaccurate claims”](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/21/anthropic-ceo-trump-sacks-woke.html) about Anthropic’s policy stances after David Sacks said the “real issue” is “Anthropic’s agenda to backdoor Woke AI.”
    
-   Prince Harry and 800+ public figures sign a Future of Life Institute statement urging a [ban on AI superintelligence](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/innovation/ai-superintelligence-ban-from-prince-harry-to-steve-bannon-unlikely-c-rcna238747) development until it can be deployed safely.
    
-   A look at the social debates in [Chile over AI investments](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/20/technology/chile-ai-politics.html), emblematic of clashes happening globally over balancing economic growth with environmental concerns.
    
-   Senate Republicans share an [AI-generated video](https://apnews.com/article/schumer-artificial-intelligence-republicans-shutdown-47bbb9434ded8823f9f4e8c5773190ac) falsely depicting Chuck Schumer saying on camera comments he made in a print interview on the government shutdown.
    
-   And US military has adopted an aggressive push to embrace AI, while the top US Army commander in South Korea says [“Chat and I”](https://www.businessinsider.com/even-top-generals-are-looking-to-ai-chatbots-for-answers-2025-10) have become “really close lately.”
