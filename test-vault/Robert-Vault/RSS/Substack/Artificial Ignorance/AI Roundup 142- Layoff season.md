### Layoff season

Even as core economic data suggests that AI is not having as big of an impact on the economy as previously predicted, it’s hard to ignore the headlines of [Fortune 500 companies](https://www.wsj.com/economy/jobs/white-collar-jobs-ai-324b749c) shedding tens of thousands of jobs.

**The big picture:**

-   Untangling the root cause here is messy: even if AI isn’t replacing those workers _today_, that doesn’t stop CEOs from doing layoffs in the hope that AI can retroactively replace them _tomorrow_ (though we’re already seeing some [cautionary tales](https://www.forbes.com/sites/quickerbettertech/2025/05/18/business-tech-news-klarna-reverses-on-ai-says-customers-like-talking-to-people/) here).
    
-   It’s also likely that [many of these layoffs](https://www.geekwire.com/2025/its-culture-amazon-ceo-says-massive-corporate-layoffs-were-about-agility-not-ai-or-cost-cutting/) are the result of some combination of pandemic-era overhiring and an increasingly complex macroeconomic environment - though the political climate may make it challenging to say the latter part publicly.
    
-   But it is clear that we’re seeing companies attempt to adopt AI usage (if not outright AI automation) at a far faster speed than previous technology shifts - leaving less time for workers to retrain and labor markets to adjust, and potentially creating acute economic and social disruption.

### OpenAI is for profits now

OpenAI completed a [major restructuring](https://openai.com/index/built-to-benefit-everyone/) that has its nonprofit arm owning 26% (though still retaining control) of the for-profit company. The deal also restructured Microsoft’s ownership agreement, with the tech giant retaining 27% ownership, [valued at $135 billion](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/openai-restructuring-company-structure-chatgpt-invest-own-rcna240138).

**Between the lines:**

-   The agreement reveals both companies [hedging their AGI bets](https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2025/10/28/the-next-chapter-of-the-microsoft-openai-partnership/): Microsoft can now pursue AGI independently or with other partners, while OpenAI gains freedom to release open-weight models and shop around for compute infrastructure - though Microsoft does retain IP rights through 2032, and OpenAI has committed to $250B in Azure spend.
    
-   The political dimension proved crucial: OpenAI appears to have gotten regulatory approval for the deal by promising to [remain in California](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/openais-promise-to-stay-in-california-helped-clear-the-path-for-its-ipo-3af1c31c) and emphasizing how AI is currently “the economic heart” of the state’s economy.
    
-   Ultimately, the deal is preparing for a future where OpenAI operates as a standalone public company with multiple partners rather than as Microsoft’s captive AI lab - as reports suggest OpenAI is preparing for an IPO [as early as late 2026](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/openais-promise-to-stay-in-california-helped-clear-the-path-for-its-ipo-3af1c31c), valuing the company at up to $1 trillion.

### The five trillion dollar company

On the heels of its [GTC in DC](https://www.nvidia.com/gtc/dc/) event, Nvidia has become the world’s first company to reach a [$5 trillion market capitalization](https://www.ft.com/content/62933c70-261c-4b7a-a045-3f9f9cceccd7), cementing its position as the most valuable corporation in history.

**Why it matters:**

-   The concentration of such enormous market value in a single chipmaker has raised questions about market stability and monopolistic dynamics in the AI supply chain, particularly as Nvidia’s fortunes remain tied to sustained enterprise spending on AI systems whose long-term returns remain unproven.
    
-   Of course, [Nvidia’s CEO pushed back](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-28/nvidia-s-huang-works-to-convince-investors-there-s-no-ai-bubble) against AI bubble concerns, announcing partnerships across industries and projecting half a trillion dollars in revenue from its latest chips - spanning humanoid robots ([Foxconn](https://www.reuters.com/business/foxconn-deploy-humanoid-robots-houston-ai-server-plant-2025-10-29/)), cybersecurity ([CrowdStrike](https://www.crn.com/news/ai/2025/5-big-moves-in-crowdstrike-nvidia-ai-partnership)), defense/logistics ([Palantir](https://www.reuters.com/business/nvidia-teams-with-palantir-go-after-corporate-logistics-business-2025-10-28/)), and pharma ([Eli Lilly](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/eli-lilly-partners-with-nvidia-to-build-ai-supercomputer-for-drug-discovery-e37247b3)), aiming to broaden its revenue base as competition intensifies.
    
-   And the conference’s patriotic messaging and Trump references reflect Nvidia’s delicate geopolitical balancing act: championing American manufacturing while pursuing “sovereign AI” deals in Europe and seeking White House help to restore access to the Chinese market that’s cost them billions in lost revenue.
    

**Elsewhere in Nvidia:**

-   Nvidia [invested in 59 AI startups](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-29/nvidia-is-accelerating-its-ai-startup-investing-spree) in 2025 so far, including 10 in the past two months, surpassing 55 investments in 2024 and 12 investments in 2022.
    
-   Jensen Huang says the US needs [“finesse” to maintain its AI lead](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-28/us-needs-finesse-to-stay-ahead-of-china-in-ai-nvidia-ceo-says) over China, and Nvidia is in an “awkward place” as President Trump meets Xi Jinping later this week.
    
-   The company unveiled [BlueField-4 data processing unit](https://www.crn.com/news/components-peripherals/2025/nvidia-reveals-bluefield-4-dpu-packed-with-64-core-grace-cpu-for-ai-data-centers), combining a 64-core Grace CPU and ConnectX-9 SuperNIC for 800 Gbps of network throughput, launching in 2026.
    
-   Nvidia’s Blackwell GPUs are now in [full production in Arizona](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/28/nvidia-jensen-huang-gtc-washington-dc-ai.html), after being solely manufactured in Taiwan previously.
    
-   And Nvidia announced [NVQLink](https://www.wsj.com/articles/nvidias-new-product-merges-ai-supercomputing-with-quantum-d0864f06), a high-speed interconnect that is the “Rosetta Stone connecting quantum and classical supercomputers.”
    

**Elsewhere in AI infrastructure:**

-   Google, Meta, and Microsoft collectively spent nearly [$80B on AI infrastructure](https://www.ft.com/content/86bb929f-e0ec-4e50-b429-e9259c3834e2) in Q3, dividing the market on whether they can translate huge capex into income.
    
-   The AI buildout, surpassing railroads and the internet, moves Big Tech from being [asset-light to asset-heavy](https://www.sparklinecapital.com/post/surviving-the-ai-capex-boom), which has historically produced inferior returns.
    
-   Sam Altman says OpenAI has committed to spending about [$1.4T on infrastructure](https://www.axios.com/2025/10/28/openai-1-trillion-altman) so far, equating to roughly 30GW of data center capacity.
    
-   An analysis of 30+ US data center proposals in 14 states found that in most cases, local officials signed [NDAs and worked with shell companies](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/data-center-ai-google-amazon-nda-non-disclosure-agreement-colossus-rcna236423) to hide details.
    
-   And Indonesia and other developing countries are becoming [AI hubs](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/its-not-just-rich-countries-techs-trillion-dollar-bet-on-ai-is-everywhere-1781a117) amid the multitrillion-dollar spending boom, in part driven by an “AI decolonization” philosophy.
