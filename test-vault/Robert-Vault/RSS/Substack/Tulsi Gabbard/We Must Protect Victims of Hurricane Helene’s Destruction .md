I’ve been here on the ground in Georgia in the wake of Hurricane Helene’s destruction to meet with survivors and officials to ensure we get life saving resources and supplies to those who need it now. I’m going to North Carolina this morning and will update you again from there.

This disaster has taken more than 190 lives across six states. Search-and-rescue efforts are ongoing, and many people are still waiting for access to electricity, communications, and water. **[We must stand with our fellow Americans now and help deliver resources, supplies, and the information they need to survive.](https://secure.anedot.com/we-must-protect/hurricanehelene1/?utm_campaign=wmp-hurricanehelene1&utm_medium=email&utm_source=nbhf)**

My non-profit organization We Must Protect took action to support the victims of the devastating Maui fires last year. We are 100% volunteer-run and are focused on how to reach those on the ground in their most urgent time of need — especially when government bureaucracy and red tape stand in the way.

We are organizing now to support survivors of Hurricane Helene's destruction. If you can, please give to support our efforts:

[DONATE](https://secure.anedot.com/we-must-protect/hurricanehelene1/?utm_campaign=wmp-hurricanehelene1&utm_medium=email&utm_source=nbhf)

I'll keep you updated on everything we're doing for immediate assistance, as well as the long road ahead when these Americans move into recovery mode.

Thank you for your help, your aloha, and your compassion.
