He built a life out of being liked.

That’s the part nobody warns men about: you can be “nice” and still be absent. You can be funny and still be selfish. You can be easygoing and still be a coward.

I knew a man like that.

Always smooth. Always joking. Always the guy who could make a room laugh.

He wasn’t a criminal. He wasn’t a monster. He wasn’t “abusive.”

He was something worse in a quieter way:

He was a **character**.

And characters don’t lead homes. They entertain them.

His wife stopped arguing years before he noticed.

Not because she found peace.

Because she gave up on being heard.

Men think the danger is when a wife complains.

It isn’t.
