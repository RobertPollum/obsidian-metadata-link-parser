You’re not as special as you think.

Good.

Because “I’m called to something big” has become the cleanest way to hide from plain obedience.

* * *

## THE VALIDATION YOU’VE BEEN FEEDING YOURSELF

God is waiting on your voice.  
God needs your uniqueness.  
God needs your platform.

## THE DEMOLITION

He doesn’t.

He never did.

And if that offends you, it proves the problem.

* * *

## THE CULTURE THAT INFECTED YOU

Every kid is a prodigy.  
Every adult is “misunderstood.”  
Every Christian is “anointed.”  
Everyone’s one viral post from “impact.”

Even politics is cosplay now—Candace Owens just made Charlie Kirk sound like Neo from the Matrix.

Chosen one energy.

And Christians eat it up.

Because deep down, we want to believe we’re the exception.

We’re not.

**Binary. No middle ground:**

-   You’re either a servant
    
-   Or you’re using “calling” to baptize your ego
    

Pick one. You already have.

* * *

## THE KILLER QUESTION

When did you stop being hungry for Him and start being hungry for applause?

Sit with that.

Don’t slide past it.

When?

* * *

## ESSENCE VS. CORRUPTION

**Real devotion looks like this:** showing up when no one sees, obeying when it costs, consuming the Word because you need it—not because you’ll post about it.

**Corrupt devotion looks like this:** reading for content, praying for the feeling, serving for the recognition, waiting for the “big assignment” while ignoring the one in front of you.

One feeds your soul.  
The other feeds your brand.

You know which one you’ve been doing.

* * *

Jonah wanted to be great in Israel.

The right crowd.  
The safe crowd.  
The crowd that claps.

Then God showed up:

_“Go to Nineveh.”_

Anywhere but there.

Nineveh was the enemy.  
Jonah wanted a platform. God wanted repentance.

So Jonah ran.

Boat. Storm. Pagan sailors. Three days in a fish.

That’ll rearrange a man.

Then Jonah finally goes.

And God uses him for exactly **8 words**:

_“Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown.”_ (Jonah 3:4)

That’s it.

No eloquence.  
No branding.  
No strategy.  
No “anointed delivery.”

**Eight words.**

120,000 people collapsed in repentance.  
The king stepped off his throne and sat in dirt.

From a reluctant prophet who didn’t even want to be there.

* * *

## THE BRUTAL TRUTH

God didn’t need Jonah’s ambition.  
God didn’t need Jonah’s personality.  
God didn’t need Jonah’s “gift.”

He used Jonah’s bare obedience.

He used a donkey to rebuke Balaam.  
A shepherd boy to kill a giant.  
A fish to drag a prophet back.

He could use a rock if He wanted.

**Screenshot this:**

_God doesn’t need you. You desperately need Him._

That sentence should hurt.

If it doesn’t, you’re still playing the hero in a story where you’re barely a footnote.

* * *

You’ve been calling it “waiting on God’s timing.”

It’s fear.

You’ve been calling it “discernment.”

It’s disobedience with religious cologne.

You’ve been calling it “calling.”

It’s ego with a Bible verse attached.

Call it what it is.

* * *

I’m not preaching at you.

I’m confessing.

I’ve been Jonah.

Chased platforms while my wife asked when I’d be present.  
Built audiences while my kids wondered if Dad loved his phone more than them.  
Measured my “impact” while the assignment right in front of me rotted.

One year I read the Bible through 30 times.

Sounds holy. It wasn’t.

It was obsession. Flesh dressed as devotion. Pride wearing a prayer shawl.

I used Scripture as content. As fuel for my personal brand. As proof I was “serious about God.”

I was serious about me.

Even good things become idols when they’re about YOU.

I had to be broken to see it.

_And brother—if you’re tired of performing, there’s grace for that too._

* * *

Five years from now, you’ll either look back and say:

“That was when I stopped performing and started surrendering.”

Or you’ll still be chasing platforms, still waiting for the “big assignment,” still dry, still hungry, still wondering why God feels distant.

He’s not distant. You’re just facing the wrong direction.

* * *

## THE FRAMEWORK

1.  **Focus on consuming Him** — not on being seen consuming Him
    
2.  **Balance between obsession and presence** — not balance between God and the world
    
3.  **Read to be fed** — not read to flex
    

This changed everything.
