I thought sharing some of my research here might be fun. This semester, I wrote a paper on Christological method, essentially exploring how scholars have approached the question of Jesus’ divine (or non-divine) identity in academic theology. While the paper itself took about six weeks to write, the ideas had been simmering in my mind for months. I checked out around 20 books from the library and downloaded another 40 to 50 scholarly articles, books, and essays to thoroughly investigate the topic. It was a slow and demanding process; each section required one to two weeks of intensive reading just to get a solid grasp of the field.

I wouldn’t recommend anyone reading the whole paper unless you love footnotes, arguments about Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek grammar, and the occasional detour into German or French scholarship. However, I think a few sections might be helpful for those interested in Christology or theological method. Additionally, you can get a feel for how I write and think in an academic setting.
