**From Jon Haidt, Zach Rausch, and Alexa Arnold**

When _The Anxious Generation_ came out in March 2024, we expected that many parents would read it, but we had no idea how quickly the world was going to change. By the end of 2025, institutions, governments, and communities around the world began to respond to alarms parents had been raising for years.

As Jon wrote in a recent _New York Times_ [article](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/08/special-series/jonathan-haidt-smartphone-ban-school.html), 2025 is a turning point for the movement to reclaim childhood. If you’re an _After Babel_ subscriber, we know you share our concern about how technology is affecting kids. You may have children or grandchildren yourself — and you may sometimes feel like you’re in an uphill battle against the tech companies, devices, and screentime. But the tide is turning, and we are filled with hope as we look back at some of the incredible progress the world has made toward rolling back the phone-based childhood this year. To name just a few of those wins:

-   We witnessed spectacular steps forward in policy-change: Across the United States, 40 [states](https://www.anxiousgeneration.com/policy-state-map) have now enacted or advanced [phone-free school legislation](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/how-to-and-how-not-to-mandate-phone); in Brazil, all schools went [phone-free nationwide](https://apnews.com/article/brazil-bill-phones-schools-restrictions-20b95516d6e2a0f63ebb642defba964b). Everyday now, teachers share with us anecdotes about kids laughing in the hallways, playing games at the lunch table, being [more attentive in class](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/11/technology/school-phone-bans-indiana-louisiana.html), and [reading more books](http://jefferson.kyschools.us/o/trunnell/article/2390148).
    
-   On Dec. 10, Australia turned one of the four norms from _The Anxious Generation_ into a landmark law: They set an [age minimum](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/australias-new-social-media-regulations) that prevents anyone under the age of 16 from entering into contracts with tech platforms that can exploit or harm them. Similar proposals are now being advanced in Brazil, Denmark, and Malaysia.
    
-   Cultural change is moving just as quickly to restore the play-based childhood. Families around the globe are [giving their children](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/kids-freedom-smartphones) more freedom to roam in the real world and [setting up landline pods](https://www.theatlantic.com/family/archive/2025/06/landline-kids-smartphone-alternative/683203/). Many Gen Z’ers themselves are [speaking out](https://www.afterbabel.com/t/voices-of-gen-z) and [pushing back](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/time-to-refuse).
    

As this work has scaled, so has the need for connective leadership across research, culture, and policy-change efforts. [Alexa Arnold](https://www.anxiousgeneration.com/team) joined us right after the book debuted to build our social impact approach, focused on driving policy, culture and behavior change across the globe. Behind the scenes, we are meeting this moment through our three interconnected entities: [The Anxious Generation Movement](https://www.anxiousgeneration.com/) (social impact), the [Tech & Society Lab at NYU Stern](https://www.anxiousgeneration.com/research) (research), and this publication, _After Babel_. Together, and working closely with [our partners,](https://www.anxiousgeneration.com/aligned-orgs) our team of researchers, strategists, communicators, and policy experts have expanded on the insights and solutions of _The Anxious Generation_ and translated them into real-world change, always grounded in rigorous science. Beyond contributing to the wins above, we’ve been working tirelessly to add fuel to the rapidly spreading movement:

-   _The Anxious Generation_ hit more than 85 weeks on the _New York Times_ bestseller list, becoming a cultural and political touchstone and mobilizing tens of thousands of parents, teachers, and young people.
    
-   Just yesterday, we released _[The Amazing Generation](https://amazinggeneration.com/)_ — the new companion book for kids — putting practical, empowering tools directly into families’ hands and helping children choose life IRL over life online.
    
-   We formally established the [Tech and Society Lab at NYU Stern](https://techandsocietylab.org/), dedicated to understanding how digital technologies reshape childhood and adolescence. The lab [published](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5224958) [several](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5224958) [working](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5256747) [papers](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5196540) and [essays](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/industrial-scale-snapchat) [engaging](http://v) [directly](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/industrial-scale-harm-tiktok?utm_source=publication-search) with debates over social media and youth mental health, while also expanding its scope well beyond that question.
    
-   We published extensively on _After Babel_ and in [the](https://www.thefp.com/p/taking-back-childhood-from-phones-jonathan-haidt) [popular](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2025/08/kids-smartphones-play-freedom/683742/) [press](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/06/18/opinion/parents-smartphones-tiktok-facebook.html), examining a wide range of topics, including [online gambling](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/smartphone-gambling-is-a-disaster), [video games](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/its-not-just-a-game-anymore?utm_source=publication-search), and the [rapid spread of](http://v) [AI chatbots](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/dont-give-your-child-an-ai-companion).
    
-   We laid the groundwork for several major releases coming in 2026, including a feature article for _The World Happiness Report_ that addresses social media’s causal impact on youth mental health, and a new public resource compiling and analyzing dozens of internal studies conducted inside Meta, many of which have garnered little prior media attention.
    

Here on _After Babel_, we published 67 articles by 66 distinct authors (several essays were co-authored), reaching hundreds of thousands of readers around the globe. Below we’ve included our top 10 most-read essays from 2025 — we hope you’ll revisit and share them.

Before we close out this remarkable year, we want to thank all 172,000+ of you for reading _After Babel_, for supporting this movement, and for being part of a growing global effort to understand and shape the future of childhood in the digital age.

Our operation, including _After Babel_, is completely powered by philanthropy. If you’re excited about what we’ve done and where we’re headed and would like to make a year-end gift, we invite you to do so at [anxiousgeneration.com/donate](http://www.anxiousgeneration.com/donate).

We wish you all a happy new year!
