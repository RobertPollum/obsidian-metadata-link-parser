When I finally laid out my vision for Bible Unbound, the first question I was asked was, "What do you mean by the Gospel?" They were referring to the tagline, which operates as a window into the channel's central tenets: We Are Here to Discover the Bible and Uncover the Gospel in Every Biblical Story.

Urg. I have always hated that question. I've been asked about it before in other contexts, but it always gives me the same pause. We need better answers than Creation, Fall, and Redemption. Those don't get to the heart of goodness, truth, and unity. In the video above, I try to revive an ancient way of communicating the fullness and simplicity of the Gospel.

Unfortunately, everyone has a slightly different version of the Gospel that they think of when encountering the word in the wild. For instance, let me ask you what you think the main message of the Gospel is. Do you think of the forgiveness of sins, perhaps the crucifixion and resurrection? Maybe you imagine Jesus riding triumphantly into Jerusalem on a donkey.

The problem with this confusion is…multidimensional, to say the least. Without a clear grasp of the Gospel, we get a breakdown in community, heretical teachings, abuses of power, and idolatry. The problem with this confusion is quite literally catastrophic.

When we clarify the Gospel, we embody the flourishing God intended in the Garden. This is the essential message of Isaiah 32 and 61. We become people of love, empathy, and justice. Therefore, we must have a coherent and historical understanding of the Gospel.

At this point, I would encourage you to watch the video above if you haven't already.

Unfortunately, I think, broadly speaking, we have lost the Gospel's fundamental meaning. While the good news is literally that Jesus is king, this means very little unless we understand that we can be united to Him.

If we are united to Christ, then everything changes. Our righteousness no longer becomes ours; our power no longer becomes ours. Everything we have and everything we are is a trust granted to us because we are united to the Heir of all things.

Union with Christ is also why our suffering is given purpose. After all, "we have been united with him in a death like his" (Romans 6:5). Our afflictions, seasons of wilderness, times of selflessness, and the small deaths we die to ourselves every day are reflections of His glory, His resurrection.

When we are united to Christ, we can also make sense of the grace we have been given. As the Father begets the Son in love, we are generated the same. Therefore, the grace that the Father gives to the Son overflows into our lives and becomes the very forgiveness, generosity, and justice we put out into the world (c.f., Matt 6:14-150).

Finally, we will be "united with him in a resurrection like his." (Rom 6:5). In other words, we are raised to new life in glory—not because of anything we have done but because of who Christ is.

In conclusion, we may say that the Gospel is good news because Jesus is King, and He is Good. Out of this Goodness, He invites us to be united with Him in a way that was lost in the Garden of Eden. That is very good news.
