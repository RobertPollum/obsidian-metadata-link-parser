The closing words of John’s life are a lightning strike in view of his life as a whole. For what was enough in John’s eyes previously? He desired to sit at the very right hand of God! Now, so decrepit he’s unable to walk, he finds that a self-sacrificial love is enough to honor the Lord.

Moreover, I wonder what it was like in the halls of that ancient Ephesian house. The believers there did not think John was going to die (Jn 21:22-23). But after years of sitting on that salty island, John’s body failed. He may have even been an unsightly interruption to their gatherings at times: falling asleep mid-sermon, snoring loudly, and with a slight odor. But he kept their attention rightly and constantly focused on Love.

Secondly, I felt it was important to at least add a small snippet about Polycarp and Ignatius. These two represent an unbroken bridge of apostolic authority stretching back to Jesus’ own ministry. It’s hard to imagine, but some early church fathers were actual contemporaries with Paul and others.
