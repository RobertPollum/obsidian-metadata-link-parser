One of the most difficult lines I have to walk on my YouTube channel is the knife’s edge between Content and Art. Even referring to many of my videos as Art feels wrong. Yet, how I understand Art theologically fits somewhere into this category.

If you would like to help me create the next piece of Bible Unbound Art, stick around to the end.

#### **Art is the material stuff of Beauty.**

Years ago, I read a book that I think changed my life in ways I only now realize. The book was Robert Adams’ _Beauty in Photography_. A tract of about 150 pages that crafts a definition of beauty which has stuck with me. In the book, Adams defines Beauty as the orderliness of chaos. Adams goes on to say that the more Beautiful something is, the more True it also has to be (and vice-versa). Therefore, something is only wholly Beautiful when it is wholly True, which Adams seems to think is Divine or Belief itself.

While it’s difficult for me to know just how religious Adams was, his view on Beauty certainly harmonizes with the Bible. This relationship between Truth and Beauty via Order is established in the beginning. There, God orders the chaos to make everything (Gen 1:1). By declaring that this Order is very Good (Gen 1:31), God makes clear that the Ordered creation contains a high degree of Beauty.

Therefore, one might say that the Artist is responsible for echoing the Beauty (the Order) of creation. “From Beauty you are, to Beauty you shall return” the Artist says. By doing this, the Artist crafts Beauty in proportion to Truthfulness

Thus, the supreme-end of Art is Order, which contains some aim of Beauty and Truth.

To be sure, Art-making can take a painfully long time. Legend has it that Ansel Adams once camped out in the desert for three days so that the lighting would be just right on a tree.

Seeing a toilet on display in a museum may not seem like Art. But, when put into its proper context (i.e., trying to say that modern art is crap) a certain Beauty arises out of the sense it makes of your world. This is because suddenly, things are a bit more ordered.

#### **However, we live in a world of Content.**

At its worst, Content is the Babylonian inversion of what is Beautiful. Unfortunately, we don’t have the space to do this in detail but we see this kind of inversion occur throughout the Bible. Perhaps no more obviously than in Esther and Daniel. There, we find a great king who hosts lavish banquets in his garden (Es 1:1-9). Yet, he fundamentally lacks the magnanimity that would make him anything worthwhile. Thus in Daniel, these kings are swapped in and out, one-for-the-other, like trading cards. In contrast, God is the magnanimous King who hosts all in His Garden and is constant and unchanging.

In the same way, we often confuse Content with Art in Babylon. In some sense, as in the other examples, there is a blurry middle. Yet, Content is fundamentally different from Art because the aim is entirely different.

The supreme-end of Content is Efficiency. Content creators are highly incentivized to produce as much content as efficiently as possible (regardless of Beauty) because it gets more people stuck on the app. And if more people are stuck on the app on your particular channel, you make more money. This is why YouTubers always talk about the algorithm and use AI to produce tons of content.

Efficiency differs from Order because its priorities are different. Efficiency prioritizes speed whereas Order (ultimately) prioritizes God Himself.

This distinction becomes obvious when the Content creator is met with an obvious problem. Let’s say that the Content creator is editing her video and finds that something she says may lead someone into sin. Does she take the time to correct the problem—maybe even requiring a few extra hours of research—or is she on a deadline?

This distinction becomes a blurry middle when Content creators are speaking Truth. A great example of this is Ruslan KD. He creates so much stuff so fast it’s amazing at how Truthful he often can be—making sense of the world for hundreds of thousands every day!

However, since the ultimate aim of Content is Efficiency, all Ruslan can do on his YouTube channel is create _good Content._ It will never be Art—it isn’t trying to be. Ruslan is a great Content creator. At its best, Content is Efficient Truth—not Ordered Truth

As you may be thoroughly confused right now, I also have recently thoroughly confused these categories. Which is why this topic has been on my mind. The issue comes down to exactly this playlist I have linked below.

These videos are the birth of Bible Unbound. And (I think) it is good Content (Efficient Truth). I made these videos before I had my degree and before I knew how to conduct research. It has a handful of typos and I can barely understand myself. Still, what I was trying to do was make sense of the scriptures for new Christians. In a way that I wish I had when I was younger. It may seem that the aim was Order, but I prioritized Efficiency. If I hadn’t, I never would have begun the channel—I would have waited another 10 or 20 years!
