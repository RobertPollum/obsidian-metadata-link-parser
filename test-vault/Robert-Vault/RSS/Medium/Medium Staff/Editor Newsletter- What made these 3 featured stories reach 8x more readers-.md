### Quick reminder: add five topics to stories before you publish

As an editor, you have a simple way to help your writers get more reads on their stories: **add up to five topics for every story in your publication.**

Adding up to five topics can help stories be presented to up to 8x as many readers on Medium, compared to stories with only one topic. When a story is published with topics, those topics help Medium’s recommendation system matchmake the story to the right reader.

Other factors play a role in recommending stories to readers, like reading history, follows, and what the story is about, but adding up to five relevant topics is one of the easiest and simplest ways to help.

Wondering which topics to add? Here’s [a guide on how to find the best topics](http://medium.com/medium-handbook/how-to-use-topics-on-medium-77883195afb3) to increase visibility on stories.

### Featured stories that performed better than average

As an editor, you can [feature a limited number of stories](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/28221990368791-How-to-feature-a-story-in-your-publication) to your publication’s followers every month. Think of these as your pub’s cover stories.

When you feature a story, a few things happen in the Medium backend:

-   All publication followers get a notification about the story
-   The story gets a bump in Medium’s story recommendation system, which shows it to more of your publication’s followers
-   The story gets a badge on it in Medium, indicating you featured it

We introduced this ability to help editors showcase the best of the best to their publication’s followers. If you pick a story you know your audience will love, that little nudge in Medium’s backend can help it reach your pub’s followers more effectively.

By contrast, if you feature a story that your audience isn’t interested in, not much will happen. Medium’s system will show it to followers, but can’t force them to click and read.

These editors featured a story they knew their audience would love, and it shows.

-   “[How Hurricane Melissa Exposed the Physics of a Broken Planet](https://medium.com/the-environment/how-hurricane-melissa-exposed-the-physics-of-a-broken-planet-6d372c066a06),” by [Ricky Lanusse](https://medium.com/u/e826713e3e3), and featured by the editors of [The Environment](https://medium.com/the-environment). This story was read by about **8.6x more readers** than similar, unfeatured stories.
-   “[A Blue Jays Fan’s Retrospective on the Dodgers-Blue Jays Series](https://medium.com/the-press-box/a-blue-jays-fans-retrospective-on-the-dodgers-blue-jays-series-81c4bb08be83),” by [Sturg Writes It Down](https://medium.com/u/3d0d0a3d94e5), and featured by the editors of [PRESS BOX](https://medium.com/the-press-box). This story was read by about **9.4x more readers** than similar, unfeatured stories.
-   “[Loneliness at 29: My Invisible Wall Story](https://soulkitchen.stackingverse.com/loneliness-at-29-my-invisible-wall-story-3217697d8f04),” by [Sergey Sokulsky](https://medium.com/u/1b448ebab55) and featured by the editors of [Soul Kitchen](https://soulkitchen.stackingverse.com). This story was read by about **8.7x more readers** than similar, unfeatured stories.

For context, here are some baseline stats across the board for all featured stories last month.

-   Featured stories were **presented** 1.8x more
-   Featured stories were **viewed** 2.1x more
-   Featured stories were **read** the same amount

This data compares the performance of featured stories versus unfeatured stories in the same publication, and from the same writer when possible.

The three examples I spotlighted demonstrate editors who really know what their publication’s followers value.

#### Daily publish limit

We’re experimenting with limiting how many stories writers can publish per day to help reduce low-quality stories on Medium.

This new limit came in part because we heard from editors that you were getting inundated with low-quality submissions, making it harder to edit stories in your pub.

As we experiment with these limits, we want to hear from you: Does the new limit help you spot writers who are mass-producing low-quality stories? How does it affect your editing process? Let us know.

Our data shows that the vast majority of writers who publish more than two stories a day are publishing stories that are not valuable to editors or readers on Medium. These stories can crowd out the more thoughtful, well-crafted stories our editors and readers prefer to see.

### The latest change to the Medium Partner Program

In December, we announced that writers will now be rewarded for sharing their stories via email notification with their readers.

[Read more here](https://medium.com/blog/partner-program-update-on-december-1-were-rewarding-email-notifications-4910c47d4cb7).

While we aimed [past changes to the Medium Partner Program](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) at encouraging writers to think about reaching readers beyond Medium, this change is designed to encourage writers to email their stories to their most loyal followers on Medium.

Our data shows that when more writers send their followers their best stories, Medium members are more likely to stay members.

We know many of you coach writers to get the story over the finish line, so consider adding one more step: Encourage your writers to send the story out to their followers.
