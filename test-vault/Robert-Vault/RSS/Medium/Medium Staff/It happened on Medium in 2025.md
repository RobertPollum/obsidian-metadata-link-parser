#### Brand-new topics, trends across the platform, celebrating new writers, and building community.

![](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*RuMZyG-tbT9ZO1SJq_LBaQ.jpeg)

Image created by [Emily Kelly](https://medium.com/u/4eb21d6eb443) // the Medium brand team

How hard is it to just start writing? According to the 5.6 million unpublished drafts created on Medium this year by writers who had an idea, started to tell it, but then stopped for some reason, it can be pretty daunting.

But the 300,000+ writers who published something on Medium for the first time this year prove it’s possible. All you have to do is sit down and tell your story.

For this final [It Happened on Medium](http://medium.com/tag/it-happened-on-medium), we’ll share some more leaderboard-style stats like the above. But you know what? There’s so much _more_ that happens on Medium beyond just a total number of stories published or read, comments left, or phrases highlighted. The act of courageously publishing your story, that moment when you read a story that turns a little piece of your world upside down — those go beyond numbers. We hope to spotlight and celebrate some of those moments, too.

In this year-end recap, you’ll get just a taste of all the weird, wild, wacky, and wonderful stories that writers shared on our platform this year, in addition to some fun stats and numbers. Turn off your notifications, turn on some reading music, and let’s take a tour through Medium in 2025.

### A few Medium firsts

Can you believe there was once a world that predated vibe coding? User [evoailabs](https://medium.com/u/c3a25b89bfc3) was the first writer to publish [a story](https://medium.com/@evoailabs/from-vibe-coding-to-vibe-doing-any-work-new-discussion-8048a5159c12) with the topic tag [vibe coding](https://medium.com/tag/vibe-coding) on Medium in February 2025, though far from the last. Since that first story, there have been nearly 6,000 stories with that topic, and over a million reads on stories with that topic tag.

Many writers also came to Medium to write about [New York mayor Zohran Mamdani](http://medium.com/tag/zohranmamdani) for the first time. Writer [Kristen Wen](https://medium.com/u/07c2a59459fa) published the [first story on Medium](https://medium.com/advanced-reporting-the-city/zohran-mamdani-wants-to-change-new-york-can-the-city-afford-it-41a83bb20447) with that topic back in May 2025, in the publication [Advanced Reporting: The City](https://medium.com/advanced-reporting-the-city). Since then, over 500 more stories have been published on that topic.

And of course, I’d be remiss if I didn’t mention that Draft Day 2025, the topic we used to track stories published in our own writing challenge, [Draft Day 2025](https://medium.com/tag/draft-day-2025), got close to 400 stories and over 50,000 reads. VP of Content [Scott Lamb](https://medium.com/u/11ba4fd53be0) had the honor of publishing [the inaugural story](https://medium.com/blog/your-story-matters-join-us-on-april-25th-for-draft-day-2025-923a150133f4), no surprise, but [Candy Keane](https://medium.com/u/5082a46897f2) published [the first actual story](https://medium.com/@candykeane/what-ive-learned-about-medium-so-far-f273363ca4c4) in the topic that was a story, not a Draft Day announcement. I liked what she had to say about Medium: “I’m not on here playing the numbers game…I’m just on here to write.” Write on.

### New writers on Medium

New writers come to Medium for all kinds of different reasons, to accomplish as many different goals as there are writers. The most popular topics for new writers this year were [programming](http://medium.com/tag/programming), [AI](http://medium.com/tag/AI), and [self](http://medium.com/tag/self). Other popular topics included [equality](http://medium.com/tag/equality), [history](http://medium.com/tag/history), and [politics](http://medium.com/tag/politics).

But I want to spotlight one new writer in particular, because he showcases a kind of Medium success story I love to share: Someone — not necessarily a professional writer, just someone! — comes to Medium with nothing but a really cool story to tell. No pre-existing audience, no SEO knowledge, nothing but that experience and a desire to talk about it. And then it turns out a lot of people are interested in reading that experience.

[Tom Morton-Collings](https://medium.com/u/26ecb149e340) is a [sales manager](https://www.linkedin.com/in/thomas-morton-collings-3237a830/?originalSubdomain=uk) at Autorola UK Ltd, but he’s also a lifelong music fan. When he felt like his musical horizons were narrowing with age, he made the decision to challenge himself. He listened to every single album on Rolling Stones’ 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. It took him six months of dedicated listening. Then he [wrote about it in a 138-min long story](https://medium.com/rock-nheavy/a-comprehensive-review-of-rolling-stone-magazines-500-greatest-albums-of-all-time-60e28c6d06a7). Even more impressively, he shared his judgments in just 5–10 words per album.

I wouldn’t call myself a music expert by any means (I’m a top 40 listener, alas), but I found myself by turns laughing, oddly defensive about his takes on my faves, and, of course, thoughtful about his witty, insightful asides on each album.

Morton-Collings calls himself an aspiring writer in his Medium bio, and I hope it’s OK to gently reframe that. You did something awesome, you wrote about it, and thousands of people read it. You’re a writer. Thank you for sharing your story on Medium.

More holistically, many folks came to Medium for the first time to share their thoughts or experiences on the current events that are top of mind for everyone. Here are a few of the standouts:

-   [Jasmine Mooney](https://medium.com/u/b83ce0df5f2d) wrote about [her experience being detained by ICE](https://medium.com/@jasminemooney) as a Canadian citizen. “I had a Canadian passport, lawyers, resources, media attention, friends, family, and even politicians advocating for me. And yet, I was still detained for nearly two weeks. Now imagine what this system is like for every other person in there — people who don’t have my privilege,” she wrote.
-   Mathematician [Laurel W](https://medium.com/u/9a2baa5c466d) shared her take on tariffs: [What can game theory teach us about Trump’s tariffs](https://medium.com/@laureldoesmath/what-game-theory-can-tell-us-about-tariffs-e7c0c70a897d)? “With the impending tariffs of the second Trump administration, it is crucial to remember the cost of defecting against trading partners,” she wrote.
-   Former National Park ranger [Elizabeth Villano](https://medium.com/u/aaee9059e2fc) told the story of [the first park sign that came down under the Trump administration](https://medium.com/@elizabethmilano/this-is-what-censorship-looks-like-in-a-national-park-the-first-park-sign-that-came-down-168dc5120319). “I’m watching as the sign that we carefully constructed to tell a full version of the story gets taken down, preventing people from learning from the past to make a better future,” she wrote.

New-to-Medium writers also showed up to share about whatever was on their mind about evergreen topics, too. Here are a few examples I loved over the year:

-   New York actor, speaker development and storytelling coach [Rachel-of-many-names](https://medium.com/u/90bd8de16681) wrote about [finally realizing she didn’t really want to reinvent herself](https://medium.com/@racheldebenedet/reinvent-yourself-no-thanks-9e6fb1ffce09). “​​I need to confess something: I have spent my entire life not knowing what the phrase ‘Find Myself’ means,” she shared.
-   [Mike Hoffman](https://medium.com/u/6915c633fe55) shared a vulnerable, moving story on [why men need to talk more about IVF](https://medium.com/@mfh334/why-men-need-to-start-talking-about-ivf-777ac5ace05c). “\[M\]en aren’t widely encouraged to process uncertainty, to sit with failure, or to acknowledge feelings of helplessness, all things that IVF forces them to confront head-on,” he wrote.
-   [Colin Myers](https://medium.com/u/b71ba1f87d77) asked a question I know has crossed my mind a time or two: [Are you closer to podcasters than your friends](https://medium.com/@colinmyers210/are-you-closer-to-podcasters-than-your-friends-1cdf76eb9018)? “How much time am I spending with podcasters — and is it possibly more than I spend with most people I know?” he wrote.

### The stories that found an audience off Medium

When you read a story that makes you think, it’s a natural impulse to want to share it with others. Here are some of the stories that most inspired readers to share Medium stories off Medium.

-   “[Building a Self-Improving Agentic RAG System](https://levelup.gitconnected.com/building-a-self-improving-agentic-rag-system-f55003af44c4),” by [Fareed Khan](https://medium.com/u/b856005e5ecd), in [Level Up Coding](https://levelup.gitconnected.com/). A behemoth of a long read (65 minutes!), reader [Alessandro Bruschieri](https://medium.com/u/834d961af88e) summed it up in [his reply](https://medium.com/@imbruce/this-is-one-of-the-most-ambitious-end-to-end-agentic-rag-builds-ive-seen-published-outside-of-an-c7a3781658e9) saying, “This is one of the most ambitious end-to-end agentic RAG builds I’ve seen published outside of an academic paper!”
-   “[How I Study Consistently While Working a 9–5 Full-Time Job](https://medium.com/write-a-catalyst/how-i-study-consistently-while-working-a-9-5-full-time-job-12b450e0e995),” by [Adarsh Gupta](https://medium.com/u/4323d7b9f6b1) in [Write a Catalyst](https://medium.com/write-a-catalyst). Readers see a lot of self-improvement stories on Medium; this one felt fresh. (Although, as a new parent, I did find myself agreeing with [Simon Purser](https://medium.com/u/690e8fa08d09)’s [reply](https://simonp2207.medium.com/i-hate-to-be-that-guy-31058379a7e4).)
-   “[They Know a Collapse is Coming](https://medium.com/age-of-awareness/they-know-a-collapse-is-coming-39a53e2ecd80),” by author and leadership consultant [Cliff Berg](https://medium.com/u/d52c3d1fd78). This one generated a lot of discussion in the replies, which the writer addressed in an edit to his piece.
-   “[Why I Put Pronouns on my Email Signature (and LinkedIn profile) and You Should Too](https://medium.com/gender-inclusivit/why-i-put-pronouns-on-my-email-signature-and-linkedin-profile-and-you-should-too-d3dc942c8743),” by trans non-fiction writer [Max Masure (they/them)](https://medium.com/u/92fdfe715f6). Another older story (first published in 2018) that stays relevant and useful for readers.
-   “[Medium Privacy Policy](https://policy.medium.com/medium-privacy-policy-f03bf92035c9),” by [Medium](https://medium.com/u/504c7870fdb6). Who’s sharing this? Please make yourself known. I have questions.

### Celebrating the Medium community

Writing, at its loneliest, can feel like posting into the void. But at its best, writing is a rich exchange of ideas, feelings, replies, and relationships. You share something — a thought, an idea, an experience — and you get a reply back that lets you know your writing touched someone.

There are so many ways writing builds community on Medium. Here are just a few we want to celebrate.

[Ossai Chinedum](https://medium.com/u/b1b5b5790877) wrote the story that received the most replies from readers. Over 1,500 readers weighed in on “[This Is How Your College Professor Knows You Used Chat Gpt](https://long.sweet.pub/ill-instantly-know-you-used-chat-gpt-if-i-see-this-d0a635bc0a00)”.

[Coding Nexus](https://medium.com/coding-nexus), [Activated Thinker](http://medium.com/activated-thinker/), and [Write Your World](https://medium.com/write-your-world/) are the community publications that started in 2025 and grew the most this year. Great job, editors!

Many editors also curated popular newsletters to help showcase their pub’s best stories to readers, and help their writers get more visibility. [Fabricio Teixeira](https://medium.com/u/50e39baefa55), the editor of [UX Collective](https://uxdesign.cc/), has the distinction of running Medium’s [most popular publication-run newsletter](https://medium.com/coding-nexus) in 2025, followed by the editors of [Muzli](https://medium.com/u/c6fbb86f1069) for their [weekly digest newsletter](https://medium.com/muzli-design-inspiration/newsletter).

There were also editors who went above and beyond to run and host in-person community events. I want to give special shoutouts to Slackjaw editor [Emily Menez](https://medium.com/u/9c5ff0bd7b72) for hosting [Slackjaw Live](https://www.instagram.com/slackjaw_live/), in-person comedy events in New York, as well as [The Belladonna Comedy](https://thebelladonnacomedy.com/) editors [Heidi Lux](https://medium.com/u/8d1cbc4c3fab), [Emily Kapp](https://medium.com/u/459221771c4f), [Katy Maiolatesi](https://medium.com/u/4729f051d800), and [Kristen Mulrooney](https://medium.com/u/556a88e54712) for their in-person comedy event, “The Jeggings Jubilee Show,” in Chicago this past October.

> (Side note — we want to see and celebrate these a lot more, but don’t currently have a good way of staying up to date. Please [let us know about any community-building you’re doing here](https://forms.gle/WwBwL8SuknmD3QHw9)!)

Overall, writers, editors, and readers build communities all over Medium (and off Medium!) in ways both big and small. You’re what makes Medium the best place to read and write on the internet. Thank you.

### Dispatches from the Medium desks

In 2025, we worked to help develop community and give control to writers and readers over their experience on Medium. We [developed a new stat](https://medium.com/blog/which-stories-reach-and-grow-your-audience-your-new-stats-pages-tell-you-01ae9e2f5846) to help writers understand how their stories were reaching and growing their audience. We [built a new inbox](https://medium.com/blog/publication-editors-welcome-to-your-new-submissions-inbox-7ed7f98db8bc) to help editors manage submissions to their publications. Starting in October, we began [modifying the Partner Program](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) to be more about rewarding writers who grow Medium by writing and sharing their stories.

We [improved the Featuring feature](http://medium.com/blog/making-featured-stories-even-more-visible-92c7a534929a) (no, we’re not taking comments on our naming policies) in February 2025. We hosted our [biggest and best Medium Day yet](https://medium.com/blog/medium-day-2025-150-sessions-workshops-and-events-to-help-you-just-start-writing-64f318f9f964), with over 150+ speakers and 8,000 attendees at Medium day, with attendees from 149 countries. We also [launched a new writing tool](https://coachtony.medium.com/the-straight-tk-on-mediumtk-ee2a87199241), currently available to alpha testers — more to come on this soon!

I’ll spare you the rest of the list (it’s long), but feel free to check out all our product releases [on our blog](https://medium.com/blog/all).

On the not-product-related side, we hired 13 new Medians, ending the year with 71 people on staff. We welcomed five new baby Medians to the world. We came together at two off-sites in Newport, Rhode Island, and Tucson, Arizona. We wrote about [jury duty](https://medium.com/@amynw/in-an-authoritarian-age-jury-duty-feels-revolutionary-0c5beceaf8eb), [books](https://medium.com/up-to-spec/most-memorable-sff-books-i-read-in-2025-ff4746981c0c), [music](https://medium.com/@jason-combs/the-25-best-albums-of-2025-07195ef90ec6), [recipes adapted from travel](https://blog.craigatallahfrost.com/when-in-florence-1aa24fbe33e8), [a hot take on turkey cooking](https://dimillian.medium.com/building-ios-apps-with-cursor-and-claude-code-ee7635edde24), [roadtrip planning](https://sophieaguado.medium.com/how-i-plan-for-my-familys-road-trips-254c2897a16c), [building iOS apps](https://sophieaguado.medium.com/how-i-plan-for-my-familys-road-trips-254c2897a16c), [management](https://yipe.medium.com/skill-x-will-turning-delegation-into-development-c3e7e3a195ee), [AI](https://jacob.blog/ai-writes-my-code-for-me-9b7edad5fb12), [gardening](https://medium.com/gardening-birding-and-outdoor-adventure/four-tips-for-closing-your-garden-for-the-winter-f7e7cef93043), [migrating from Kotlin 3 to 4](https://medium.engineering/medium-android-app-migrating-from-apollo-kotlin-3-to-4-lessons-learned-ff8d0d861cdb), [fidget toys](https://cassiebegins.medium.com/writing-practice-957ae47f53e0), and, of course, [writing](https://cassiebegins.medium.com/writing-practice-957ae47f53e0).
