### Featured stories typically reach 40–70% more readers

Many editors don’t realize that featuring stories helps those stories perform better on Medium. When an editor features a story, our data shows that on average, 40–70% more readers see that story compared to non-featured stories in the same publication.

Quick reminder: “Featuring” is an ability [we introduced to publication editors](https://medium.com/blog/introducing-featured-stories-for-publications-a0a714b8151d) about a year ago. When you feature a story, it does a few things:

-   The story gets a badge on Medium, indicating it’s featured
-   Followers of the publication get a notification about the featured story
-   The story is prioritized in the recommendations for that publication’s followers
-   The story is prioritized in the curation queue for review according to our [distribution guidelines](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/360006362473-Medium-s-Distribution-Guidelines-How-curators-review-stories-for-Boost-General-and-Network-Distribution)

Basically, think of it as you putting your stamp of approval on a story for your publication’s readers and writers.

I was curious to learn what made a story worthy of getting a feature, so I asked editors to let me know why they chose to feature certain stories. It mostly boiled down to four reasons:

1.  The story was relevant — either timely, or a really good fit for the publication’s mission or theme.
2.  The editor thought the story deserved a bigger audience and more eyes.
3.  The editor wanted to support the writer in some way.
4.  It was just a really fantastic story.

If you’re interested in learning more, [here is a more detailed breakdown of what those editors said](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/what-makes-editors-on-medium-feature-stories-92ea652fc1b1).

### Questions about the changes to the Medium Partner Program?

We’ve made a few [changes to how we pay writers through the Partner Program](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) recently. We know this has raised a lot of questions for Medium writers, so we’re hosting a fireside chat online to answer FAQs.

[Here’s a form to submit your question](https://forms.gle/F5dPgDDsmFPnCetQA), and [here’s where you can register for the fireside chat](https://events.zoom.us/ev/AokrfeMQwm0pckswjI4lXJF413-Y6P0D1FN_y4XMRe466lDOl4ZQ~AvRxSCSNxx4Ru-AfDMvc3JNryn4Tuk7HWnKwXS6QTR8e2gv25vfa_84oc7Qq4h0Yw_N8JTcPucHTjYyAFRWlTtHIkQ).

In the past, the Partner Program rewarded writers only when members engaged with their stories — reading, clapping, leaving replies on Medium. But there was little reward for those writers who worked to grow their own audience (and help grow Medium’s) by sharing their by sharing their stories off-platform.

We’re working to make it a more complete partnership, rewarding the writing that makes readers come to Medium, become members, and find value in their membership.

So far, we’ve shifted 5% of the budget to reward external traffic and 15% to reward search traffic. We’ll be making small monthly changes for the next four months.

### Update on issues users in India were experiencing accessing Medium

We’ve heard in the past few weeks that many Medium users based in India weren’t able to access [Medium.com](http://medium.com) or the app. To the best of our knowledge, this was due to internet service providers in India pointing to the wrong IP address.

It appears that the problem is becoming less severe, which is great news. If you’re still affected, we suggest you update your DNS, which lets you replace your ISP’s DNS (like Airtel, Jio, or Vi) with a **public resolver**, with the steps outlined on [our status page here](https://statuspage.incident.io/medium/incidents/01K8KP4JMFAJ0E852ZM6DEJWEZ).
