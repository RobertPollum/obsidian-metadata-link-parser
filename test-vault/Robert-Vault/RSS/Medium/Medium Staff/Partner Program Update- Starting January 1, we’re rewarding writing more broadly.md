#### Our members value all kinds of writing—and so do we

![An image that says: Medium Partner Program Update, January 2026](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*F6dZZc2VLcmj5bP1-g4Klw.jpeg)

Image created by [Emily Kelly](https://medium.com/u/4eb21d6eb443) // the Medium Brand team

All human writing is valuable. If you’ve ever published a story — or read one that changed your life — you know it doesn’t have to be a flawless work of craftsmanship. It just has to be meaningful.

That’s why, for [our fourth tweak](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) to our Partner Program as we work to make it a better fit for more writers, we have spread the Partner Program earnings that typically go to Boosted stories more broadly beyond Boosted stories.

### What can you expect?

On average, stories that are not Boosted will now earn more than they did before. Boosted stories will still earn at a higher rate than unboosted stories, but not as much.

### What exactly is changing?

On Medium, [our curators routinely identify stories that qualify for a Boost](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/360006362473-Medium-s-Distribution-Guidelines-How-curators-review-stories-for-Boost-General-and-Network-Distribution). This represents a tiny fraction of the stories published on Medium, but it has never represented all the valuable stories found on Medium, all the stories worth writing, reading, or sharing.

For Boosted stories, we increase the distribution, meaning Medium pushes them more widely across the platform. If the writer puts that story behind the paywall, we also add a multiplier on that story’s earnings. That story earns more than an unboosted story with a similar level of member engagement would earn.

But our members value many kinds of stories beyond those that qualify for a Boost. Boosted stories will still get wider distribution and higher earnings — but now, more earnings will go to valued stories that aren’t Boosted.

### A brief note on the value of writing

Normally we like to keep these updates short and sweet, but this one deserves a little more explanation of why we care. Why does writing matter? Why does reading matter? These are the big questions we’re all facing in 2026 and beyond.

Writing makes the world a better place, whether it’s a hastily-penned book rant, a list of things that made your 2025 better, or a note for your child’s first day back at school. When someone reads your writing, they step inside your brain and understand your perspective. That’s true for your heavily researched magnum opus, but it’s also true for a short post about a dinner recipe that made your week easier.

Writing matters, reading matters, and we want to encourage and incentivize you to share all your stories on Medium.

### Why did we make this change?

Our mission is to deepen understanding by building community around shared stories, no matter who wrote them. Those stories can be from professional writers or folks who have never published anything before but have a story to tell.

On behalf of our members, we strive to find and reward better, higher-quality writing than anywhere else on the internet. But first and foremost, we want you to write. We want editors to feel a stronger connection between their editorial efforts and how well stories perform financially for their writers. We want member activity to have a more direct impact on the earnings of writers.

Boosted stories will still get increased distribution and increased earnings. However, we hope that by spreading the earnings out across more of the shared stories from our writers, rather than concentrating those earnings so heavily on the highest quality stories, writers will feel that their stories are valued more.

### What have the previous changes been?

So far, we’ve encouraged writers to share their paywalled stories off Medium, consider how to make their stories discoverable through search, and let their most dedicated followers know when they’ve published something new through email notifications. You can read [a full list of the changes here](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e).

We hope this encourages writers to share more stories that might not qualify for a Boost but are still worth writing.

### What’s next?

We’ll continue working to develop a Partner Program that rewards writers for building and growing Medium with us by sharing their valuable stories.
