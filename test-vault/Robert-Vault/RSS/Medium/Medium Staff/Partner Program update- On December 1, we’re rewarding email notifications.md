#### Incentivizing writers to send their stories through email notifications to subscribers

![An image that says “Medium Partner Program Update: December 2025”](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*T-nor1GDGkoaBS_CLiGRdQ.jpeg)

Image created by [Emily Kelly](https://medium.com/u/4eb21d6eb443) // the Medium Brand team

How can we build a Partner Program that helps Medium grow? That’s the question we’re trying to answer with [a series of six small, monthly changes](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) to how we distribute money to stories on Medium.

So far, we’ve shifted the Partner Program budget to reward:

-   [External member traffic](https://medium.com/blog/partner-program-update-starting-october-1-were-rewarding-external-traffic-15cb28f75bc9) (anytime a member comes to your story through a channel that Medium doesn’t control; for example, if you share your story on LinkedIn)
-   [Member search traffic](https://medium.com/blog/partner-program-update-starting-november-1-were-rewarding-search-traffic-3ac0635f9f6b) (when members find your story through search engines like Google)

This month, we’re going in a slightly different direction: We’re rewarding writers who **share their stories through email notifications** on Medium.

### What does that mean?

When you publish a story on Medium, you have the option to send a preview of your story via email to anyone who is subscribed to get [email notifications](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/360059837393-Email-notifications) from you.

![A screenshot that shows the option in the publish flow of your Medium story that allows you to send email notifications to your subscribers](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/966/1*IGa_ywKBJ4fi16hIDgigOw.png)

Screenshot taken by Medium staff

They’ll get an email in their inbox with a preview of the story and a link to read the full story on Medium.

If a member comes to Medium to read that story because you sent it to them in an email notification, you’ll earn slightly more for that member engagement than if they had found that story on Medium through the app or website.

### Why are we making this change?

Medium grows when we get new members and when existing members continue their membership. Existing members continue their membership when they see the value in Medium.

When writers use email notifications more, member retention increases. This makes sense — readers who sign up to get notified when you publish something new value your writing. When they get a notification that you published a new story, they get an instant reminder about the great writing they find on Medium.

This latest change is aimed at encouraging writers to reach those readers who know and appreciate their work best — the readers who choose to subscribe to email updates about your latest work.

We hope those readers continue to see the value in their membership and come back to use Medium more frequently.

In general, with all these changes, we want to incentivize writers to think about distributing their stories. When writers publish stories that are widely shared and discovered through search, Medium grows. That’s good for Medium, and it’s good for writers.

### What should you do?

If you want to take advantage of this incentive, when you publish a story, check the box to send it to your subscribers. If those readers are Medium members, and if they choose to continue reading your story on Medium, that engagement will earn at a higher rate.

You can also [fill out your bio](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/how-and-why-to-set-up-your-medium-bio-and-about-page-db22f5bd240c) to tell readers more about yourself, your expertise, and why they should choose to follow you for more.

Our data shows that readers are more likely to follow writers with a filled-out bio. It makes sense! It’s an important piece of context that helps readers understand why they should read your story, follow you for more, and subscribe to make sure they don’t miss anything from you.

Zooming out, write stories that grow your audience. This kind of story will be different for every writer, depending on your interests, expertise, and life. You can check your [individual story stats pages](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/34831991136151-Story-s-detailed-stats-page) to see what kinds of stories tend to resonate with and grow your audience the most. On every story’s stats page, you can see how many readers chose to follow or subscribe to you through that story.

![A screenshot of a Medium story’s stats page, where you can learn how many readers chose to follow or subscribe to you through that story](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*XeuA2Kl46R8g67ORk1ziiQ.png)

Screenshot taken by Medium staff

Finally, put yourself in the reader’s shoes. When you follow a writer, why do you do so? What kinds of stories make you think, “That was so great, I want to be sure I don’t miss another”?

We’ve also done a lot of work on our end to help readers easily subscribe to story notifications from the writers they love — a recent design shift is responsible for [writers getting 30x more subscribers than before](https://medium.com/blog/how-a-design-change-is-leading-to-7x-more-email-subscribers-across-medium-19ae1d73b541).

### FAQs

Let’s get into some of the FAQs writers have been asking us about these changes.

#### Why are you doing this?

We want to reward writers who bring members to Medium through non-Medium channels, like search, email, and social. This helps Medium grow.

#### What has changed?

Before, writers earned money when members engaged with their paywalled story, regardless of how those members found the story.

Now, those stories earn more money if readers discover them through search, non-Medium channels like social media, and through email notifications.

#### What about stories that are not behind the paywall?

Stories that are not behind the paywall don’t earn money through the Partner Program.

#### Are you paying for external traffic?

Only if that traffic comes from Medium members. We do not pay for non-member traffic.

#### What’s next?

We’ll continue to experiment with ways to incentivize writers to help Medium grow.
