### The formula for success on Medium (doesn’t exist)

We see a lot of new writers come to Medium who are what I call platform-poisoned. You worry there’s a correct (and incorrect) time and day to post to get more views. You wonder if the algorithm punishes you for not publishing on a specific daily or weekly schedule, or for not sticking to your niche, that there’s a niche that gets way more money or attention than others, or that once you hit a specific number of followers, you’ll start getting treated differently by the algorithm.

I get why, and I used to be the same. On other platforms, it can be like that. Here on Medium, we have tried our best to build a recommendations system that rewards writing our readers find meaningful, and has as little to do with other factors as possible. You write a story, and it finds the readers who want to read it.

Besides writing the best story you can, three specific things can help your story find readers, and a whole lot of things don’t.

The three things that help:

-   [**Find a** **good-fit publication**](https://medium.com/blog/how-to-find-a-publication-on-medium-59527f6b7a77) to help give your story a home
-   [**Add five topics**](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/how-to-use-topics-on-medium-77883195afb3) to your story to help our system matchmake it to readers
-   [**Fill out your bio**](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/how-and-why-to-set-up-your-medium-bio-and-about-page-db22f5bd240c) so readers know why they should read your story and follow you for more

Our data shows that adding topics and getting your story accepted in a publication helps your story perform better, and that filling out your bio results in more followers.

So what _don’t_ you have to worry about? A non-exhaustive list:

-   **Sticking to a consistent schedule**. Our recommendation system does not take your publishing frequency into account whatsoever. Publish when you have something to say, whether that’s once a day, or just once, ever, and you’re done.
-   **Only publishing in your niche**. Medium is topic-driven, which means that your story will be shown to readers who may have never heard of you before. Our system doesn’t know what your “niche” is, and neither do those new readers. They only judge your story based on what they see in front of them. Existing readers of yours might be used to your photography writing and choose not to read your stories about relationships, but that’s up to them, not the system.
-   **The “best” time to publish your story.** This is for many reasons, but one you might not know is that a huge proportion of reads on Medium come from the daily and weekly digests — a collection of 10–15 stories Medium emails to readers based on their interests, geographic location, and time zone. If we think your story will interest a reader, it will land in their inbox, and they’ll read it when they check their email.
-   **The “right” formula for your titles**. Readers click on titles that are relevant to them. If the title is clickbait, they either don’t click at all, or they quickly bounce, which negatively affects your story’s performance. I [published a guide](http://medium.com/medium-handbook/a-guide-to-writing-titles-on-medium-a539b6e12818) (with a worksheet, if you’re interested) here.

I understand the worry that you don’t know the formula to make your story viral. But one of my favorite things about Medium is that there really is none. Stories do well when readers read them, and writers do well when they write the stories that mean something to them. That’s pretty much it.

I hope that makes you feel like you can just sit down and write your story. That’s what Medium readers want most.

### Writing prompts for the New Year

Looking for inspiration? Want to write but not sure what to write? You’re in luck. Here’s a curated list of prompts from publications this month.

_New to Medium? Publications are themed collections of stories, run by other Medium writers called editors. You can submit your story to a publication. Editors can give your story a polish, provide some community, and help give you and your writing a home on Medium._

**One-word prompts:**

-   The editors of [Sparkle City Magic](https://medium.com/sparklecity-creative-works) give you five words. Pick one and let yourself be inspired to write a short story, poem, or song. Here’s [January’s prompt](https://medium.com/sparklecity-creative-works/mystical-adventure-edition-28-c78577ca0316). Check out the [submission guidelines here](https://medium.com/sparklecity-creative-works/sparkle-city-magic-6785bf4d73fa).
-   Pick a single word — your choice, it can be anything — and write 300–500 words. Here’s [the full prompt](https://medium.com/no-time/one-word-one-story-a-writing-challenge-4b45a6d7d0dc) from the publication, [No Time](https://medium.com/no-time), and here are [the submission guidelines](https://medium.com/no-time/no-time-publication-0b759cdf7126).
-   Your word is “home.” What does that mean for you? The full prompt instructions from [Intersectionality](https://medium.com/intersectionality) are [found here](https://medium.com/intersectionality/home-ca57ebc8cc46), and [read the submission guidelines here](https://medium.com/intersectionality/submission-guidelines-for-intersectionality-4a67c3843709).
-   Pick a word that you want to define your 2026, and write about it for [Everyday Being](https://medium.com/everyday-being). Read [the full prompt here](https://medium.com/everyday-being/my-2026-word-of-the-year-ad32b296284a), and check out [the submission guidelines for this publication here](https://medium.com/everyday-being/everyday-being-my-new-publication-a94309f043d5).
-   Your prompt is, fittingly, “the future.” The editors at [Promptly Written](https://medium.com/promptly-written) provide different ways to think about this in [their full prompt instructions](https://medium.com/promptly-written/its-january-and-your-first-monthly-theme-of-2026-with-promptly-written-c8b633c0b99c). Check out their [submission guidelines here](https://medium.com/promptly-written/new-pw-submission-guidelines-445d69add05e).

**For those who have thoughts about culture**:

-   How does culture survive over time? “Explore cultural survival as a living negotiation shaped by responsibility, resilience, and choice.” This one can be fiction, nonfiction, a poem, anything. Check out [the full prompt here](https://medium.com/a-culturated/a-culturateds-january-2026-writing-prompt-8baceb45f1d5) from [A-Culturated](http://medium.com/a-culturated), which includes submission guidelines.

**Reviews as prompts**:

-   Read something from the public domain and write about it for [Reviewsday Tuesday](https://medium.com/reviewsday-tuesday/)’s [January prompt](https://medium.com/reviewsday-tuesday/reviewsday-monthly-themes-january-june-2026-e355850a72b5). Read the [submission guidelines here](https://medium.com/reviewsday-tuesday/reviewsday-tuesday-submission-guidelines-2026-3f6f0afb6d9c).
-   Eat a particularly delicious or disgusting holiday snack? Write a [casual snack review](https://medium.com/casual-snack-reviews). Read the [submission guidelines here](https://medium.com/p/f5d060172c47).
-   Use a product you love, hate, or have any kind of feeling about? Write a [casual product review](https://medium.com/casual-product-reviews). The [submission guidelines can be read here](https://medium.com/p/4717cc67f80b).

### The latest update to the Medium Partner Program: rewarding writing more broadly

Read [the full announcement here](https://medium.com/blog/partner-program-update-starting-january-1-were-rewarding-writing-more-broadly-4907e149e38c). The TL;DR is that **we have spread the Partner Program earnings that typically go to Boosted stories more broadly beyond Boosted stories**.

On Medium, [our curators routinely identify stories that qualify for a Boost](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/360006362473-Medium-s-Distribution-Guidelines-How-curators-review-stories-for-Boost-General-and-Network-Distribution). This represents a tiny fraction of the stories published on Medium, but it has never represented all the valuable stories found on Medium, all the stories worth writing, reading, or sharing.

Our members value many kinds of stories beyond those that qualify for a Boost. Boosted stories will still get wider distribution and higher earnings — but now, more earnings will go to valued stories that aren’t Boosted.
