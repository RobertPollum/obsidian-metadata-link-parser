#### The next in a series of small-scale changes to make the Medium Partner Program a better fit for more writers

![](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*jB4xmL6Utpi-8aB7GrRySQ.jpeg)

Image created by [Emily Kelly](https://medium.com/u/4eb21d6eb443) // the Medium Brand team

Last month, [we announced](https://medium.com/blog/partner-program-update-starting-october-1-were-rewarding-external-traffic-15cb28f75bc9) we’ll be making and communicating small changes to the Medium Partner Program through March 2026.

In October 2025, we shifted 5% of the Medium Partner Program payouts to member activity from external traffic. This month, we’re shifting 15% of the Partner Program budget to activity on stories that members discover through search engines like Google.

Our Partner Program currently rewards the stories that our members read, reply to, and highlight. We also want to reward writers who bring readers to Medium through other channels, like email, search, and social sharing.

Writing stories that are optimized for search engines is a win-win. It brings readers to your stories, and it brings new members to Medium. That’s why we want to reward you for doing that.

### What you can do

If you want to benefit from this change, you can make your paywalled stories more discoverable to search engines like Google. The good news is that Medium is one of the best writing platforms for technical SEO, and you likely benefit from search traffic already, even if you do very little optimizing. The better news is that with some additional work, you can get even more search traffic.

In general, the best practice to optimize for search is to keep readers in mind. What do they type into Google to search for? What value can you provide to those readers? How can you make that value clear in the title of your story? Paired with Medium’s strong technical SEO, a little bit of effort can go a long way.

Many Medium writers have written helpful guides about how to rank, how to find keywords, and how to structure your story in the most optimized way. Try searching in topics like [SEO](https://medium.com/tag/seo) for topical, expert-driven SEO advice. For those who want to go deeper, Google has [a more technical guide](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide#make-your-site-interesting) on how to think about ranking in search, including a section on [how to think about what readers might search for](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide#expect-search-terms).

Additionally, check out our Medium Handbook guide on [how to edit the SEO settings on your stories](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/how-to-use-medium-seo-settings-to-reach-more-readers-97facde95e83), which search engines use as a source of information when ranking and displaying stories. This tutorial walks you through how to change the SEO title and description of your story to help make it more discoverable by search engines like Google.

### FAQs

#### Q: Why this change?

We want to improve the Partner Program to be a better fit for more writers — those who write stories our existing members value, as well as those who bring new members to Medium. In this case, we’re focusing on search traffic.

#### Q: How does this affect the previous Partner Program shift?

The previous shift to the Partner Program will still be in place.

#### Q: How does this affect non-member activity?

Non-member activity is unchanged. Non-member activity doesn’t earn any money on paywalled stories.

#### Q: What happens if a reader comes through a friend link?

It’s extremely rare for a member to find a friend link through search engines, as search engines index the standard URL. If a member did happen to come to your story through a search that brought them to a friend link, that traffic would get the same reward. Non-member activity would not get any bonus, regardless of how they come across your story, since we only pay for member activity.

#### Q: Will this affect earnings on stories published before November 1?

Yes, so feel free to go back and optimize previously published stories, or paywall any that are already getting search traffic you’d like to earn from!

If you’re interested in discovering which stories are getting search traffic, check out the traffic sources on [your story’s stat pages](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/215108608-Stats).

#### Q: What’s next?

We’ll continue to experiment with how to incentivize writing that brings in your readers from outside Medium. We’re looking forward to your feedback.
