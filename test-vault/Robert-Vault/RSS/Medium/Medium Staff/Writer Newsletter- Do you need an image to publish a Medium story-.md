### Questions about the changes to the Medium Partner Program?

We’ve made a few [changes to how we pay writers through the Partner Program](https://medium.com/@MediumStaff/list/medium-partner-program-updates-0ce55a20af6e) recently. In the past, the Partner Program rewarded writers only when members engaged with their stories — reading, clapping, leaving replies on Medium. But there was little reward for those writers who worked to grow Medium by sharing their stories off-platform.

We’re working to make it a more complete partnership, rewarding the writing that makes readers come to Medium, become members, and find value in their membership.

So far, we’ve shifted 5% of the budget to reward external traffic and 15% to reward search traffic. We’ll be making small monthly changes for the next four months.

We know this has raised a lot of questions for Medium writers, so we’re hosting a fireside chat online to answer FAQs. ### An update on the difficulties connecting to Medium for users based in India

On October 16, we started hearing from many users based in India that they couldn’t access Medium either on the web or through our Medium app. After investigating, our current understanding is that this is an issue happening at or above the Internet Service Provider (ISP) level, rather than on Medium’s side.

We’ve been happy to hear from our users that it looks like access is being restored to certain ISPs. If you’re still having difficulties, please follow the instructions [on our status page](https://status.medium.com/incidents/01K8KP4JMFAJ0E852ZM6DEJWEZ) to reach out to your ISP and update your own DNS. You can also [subscribe to the status page](https://status.medium.com/incidents/01K8KP4JMFAJ0E852ZM6DEJWEZ) to get emails when there’s an update.

What happened? As far as we are aware, traffic from a number of ISPs in India, including Jio, Airtel, and Vi, pointed to the wrong IP address for [medium.com](https://medium.com/), disrupting your access.

We’ll continue trying to get in touch with the NOC / DNS teams at the affected ISPs. If more customers contact their ISPs, the ISPs will be more motivated to address the problem on their end. And remember, for updates, [our status page here](https://statuspage.incident.io/medium/incidents/01K8KP4JMFAJ0E852ZM6DEJWEZ) is the best place to check and share. As it is not a [medium.com](https://medium.com/) URL, even affected users should be able to reach it.

### Why editors feature stories in their publications

Publication editors can [feature](https://medium.com/blog/introducing-featured-stories-for-publications-a0a714b8151d) a limited number of stories from their publication every month. This prompts Medium’s recommendation engine to recommend that story to more of that publication’s followers.

Featured stories reach more readers compared to non-featured stories in the same publication from the same writer.

Why do editors feature certain stories? And how can you write a feature-worthy story? I asked editors to tell me more.

In general, editors featured stories for one of four reasons: it was a relevant and timely piece; they felt that the story in particular deserved to reach a wide audience; they wanted to signal support for the writer of the story, or it was just a great story they thought their readers would love. [Here’s a more detailed breakdown](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/what-makes-editors-on-medium-feature-stories-92ea652fc1b1).

If you’re interested in being featured by a publication editor, your best bet is to [find a publication that’s a good fit for your writing style](https://medium.com/blog/how-to-find-a-publication-on-medium-59527f6b7a77), carefully read the submission guidelines, and write the best story you can.

### Medium 101 tutorials: images and titles

Do images help to convince readers to check out your story? And how can you write better titles? We looked at Medium data to answer both questions.

First, we found that broadly speaking, images don’t have a big impact on presentations, views, or reads. Writers choose to add images for all kinds of good reasons — to add extra context, authority, personal branding to their story — but in general, if you don’t have a good image to hand, no image is better than a bad one.

Find out more about [image best practices here](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/do-you-need-to-add-pictures-to-your-medium-stories-8401c6db7afb).

Titles do play a big role in telling readers why they should read your story. But it’s much harder to look at broad trends, because a “good” title will vary hugely between genres, publications, audiences, and even points in time. (Think, for example, of how different a great title would be between a fiction piece versus a political piece!) And of course, you want to steer away from clickbait, which can limit your distribution.

The best title advice is to study what titles in your topic are popular with readers. I walk you through how to analyze topic pages and publication archives to understand what titles are popular with their target readers. I put together [a guide to writing better titles](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/a-guide-to-writing-titles-on-medium-a539b6e12818) and [a worksheet you can fill out](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1V6wkBndQHpQ6jGj8CfC1t9hp5w4dSaUHibLnyx9joSg/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.nj06gi3480xe) to work on your Medium titles.

### Ask Medium: Can writers use their own drawings as a picture on their Medium story?

Thanks to [Rubaiyat Rahman](https://medium.com/u/17b0571ec0f0) for [asking this question](https://rubaiyatrs.medium.com/can-writers-use-their-own-drawings-as-picture-of-medium-post-3ff877017c0b)!

In short, yes! Original art is not only permitted but very much encouraged. Medium readers value anything that helps deepen their understanding of the story and the writer, and personal art absolutely counts. Examples include scientists designing their own flowcharts, digital art, original photography, handwritten notes, and more.

You can read more about this in our latest Medium Handbook post about [whether you need images on your Medium story](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/do-you-need-to-add-pictures-to-your-medium-stories-8401c6db7afb).
