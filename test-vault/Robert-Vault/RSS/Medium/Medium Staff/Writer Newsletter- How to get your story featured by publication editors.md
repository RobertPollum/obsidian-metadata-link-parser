### When should you paywall your stories on Medium?

Many writers want to know whether, when, and why they should paywall stories. Paywalling a story means only Medium members can read it, and when they do, you earn money from their engagement with it.

Writers control whether there’s a paywall on their stories. You have a few options:

-   Paywall some, all, or none of your stories
-   Paywall your stories when you publish, then remove the paywall later
-   Paywall your stories after a period of time after you publish

The short answer to the “best” strategy is that it depends on what you want to get out of writing on Medium.

I wrote up [a full guide here](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/should-you-paywall-your-medium-stories-2399a9e3a604).

### How to get your story featured

On Medium, [publication editors can feature stories](https://medium.com/blog/making-featured-stories-even-more-visible-92c7a534929a) to their publication’s followers. A featured story gets a bump in the algorithm to that publication’s followers, plus publication followers get notified about it, so it’s a great way to get more visibility on your stories.

Publication editors can only feature a limited number of stories per month, so they’re usually looking for the latest and greatest stories they think their readers would most appreciate.

If you’re wondering how to write a story that editors choose to feature, here are a few things you can do:

-   **Submit your story to a publication**. This should go without saying, but only stories in publications can be featured by publication editors!
-   Carefully **read that publication’s submission guidelines**. Make sure your story is a good fit that the publication’s readers will value.
-   **Read stories recently published** in that publication to get a sense of what the editors typically publish.
-   If you can, **check out that publication’s featured stories** to get a sense for what they feature. Many publications have a featured gallery to help writers understand what they tend to feature.
-   Read [this story about **why editors feature stories**](https://medium.com/medium-handbook/what-makes-editors-on-medium-feature-stories-92ea652fc1b1)**!** Dozens of editors wrote in to tell me about why they featured a specific story. Common themes included timeliness, relevance, craft, and a story they knew their readers would love.
-   **Focus on quality and craft**. Editors are looking for the best of the best.
-   If you don’t succeed, **try again**. Don’t be discouraged by not getting featured right away.

The bar to getting featured by an editor is high! But even trying to write a featurable story will result in a story that’s a better fit for that publication’s readers, so it’s worth the effort no matter what.

### Ask Medium: How can I add footnotes to my Medium story?

This is one of our most FAQs — if you’ve ever wondered this, wonder no more!

There are two ways to add footnotes to your story: Use our existing **inline responses** as a way to annotate your story, or use superscript.

Here’s how to use inline responses to footnote your Medium story.

1.  Highlight the word or phrase you want to annotate on a published story.
2.  Click the respond button (looks like a speech bubble) in the formatting toolbar.
3.  Type your footnote in the note field. You can add citations, little asides, any extra context that would otherwise derail your story, or anything else you like.
4.  When readers see the highlighted text, they can click to reveal your note.

Here’s [a short video demonstrating how to do this](https://www.loom.com/share/f657dcd23ac04b5c90c17b70783e9ad6).

You can also use superscript (using the ^ character and then adding a number) and then manually add in footnotes. Find [more information here](https://help.medium.com/hc/en-us/articles/215194537-Using-the-story-editor#h_01K81BT81N098CR6JCQCNJ06W2).
