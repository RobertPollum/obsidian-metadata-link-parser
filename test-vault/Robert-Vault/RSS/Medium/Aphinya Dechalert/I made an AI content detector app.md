# [I made an AI content detector app | by Aphinya Dechalert](https://freedium-mirror.cfd/https://medium.com/@PurpleGreenLemon/i-made-an-ai-content-detector-app-740a69fcf710?source=rss-3ac3fb3f09e0------2)

My day job is editing words. I send people outlines, and people send words back. As an editor, I expect these words to be coherent and filled with human-derived insights. However, more often than I'd like, I get a badly stitched-together ChatGPT output.

If you hand in AI-generated content, at least make it look human.

And that's where many 'writers' fail.

[What the app looks like.](https://theodoreapp.com/)

### I made Theodore to solve a problem

I've spent countless hours trying to explain to writers why their work is being rejected.

And it's the same thing, over and over again. Em dahses. Weirdly uniform sentences. Weirdly long sentences that tell you nothing but feel like it's telling you something. Overused AI cliches and sentence structures.

Content nowadays is dripping with AI, and as humans, we naturally start to assimilate these patterns. We start to become bots rather than overlords of the bots.

But we're still humans, and there's variability in being human. That's what makes writing engaging and interesting to read.

Theodore exists to bring this variability in being human back.

### The problem with Grammarly and Quillbot

I use Grammarly on a daily basis, and there are ways to get around its AI detector. But it doesn't wipe away the scent of AI. Cut a few words here. Drop a sentence there. Suddenly, the piece is less than 6% AI.

Then there's Quillbot. Personally, I like Quillbot. I send the AI reports to my writers when they try to convince me that humans write with em dashes all the time.

No one writes with em dashes. That's a specialty that's unique to ChatGPT content.

I'm not against ChatGPT. What I'm against is people trying to pass ChatGPT content as their own without putting in their own thoughts and opinions.

Personally, I use ChatGPT like a search engine, and then I put my own spice into the content delivered.

The problem with AI detectors is that they only detect. They don't tell you why it looks like AI and I really don't have the time to explain every time why the phrase " As technology continues to evolve" and "game changer" smells like AI.

Many writers and content creators who rely on AI to generate their content still struggle to distinguish between what's real and what's robotic. **I made Theodore to point out these problems and explain why.**

### How Theodore works

Paste or type your text into Theodore and the app will give you back a human score and a readability score.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/I%20made%20an%20AI%20content%20detector%20app%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-QB3nBVNYiH.png)

Human score is based on offense serverity. Yes. There's 2 issues but they're dripping with AI.

The human score is based on how many AI sentence patterns and phrases it finds. You can click on the highlighted parts, and the tooltip will tell you what's wrong with it.

You fix it and make it human.

The readability score is based on sentence length and structure. This feeds into how easily someone can read and understand your text. It looks at things like word complexity, passive voice, and rhythm.

I broke up the readability and human score because of the variety of content types and their target readers. You can still have a low readability score but still be human.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/I%20made%20an%20AI%20content%20detector%20app%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-BCYm9hKwPH.png)

AI writing can feed into the readability score based on sentence structure, but it's not always the case. So I kept it separate from the actual AI score so you can be the judge on its quality and context.

There's also a "What's going on with your writing" section that gives you a summary of your problem areas. This section contains helpful guidance on how to make your text better.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/I%20made%20an%20AI%20content%20detector%20app%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-y4jLPbOcaw.png)

This is the part that AI detectors don't really get into. They just give you the AI detection score and that's it. Theodore tells you why.

### Theodore isn't perfect, but it's a start

I just launched the app last night. There's still a roadmap of features I want to push out, but first, I need to road test it.

> The point of Theodore isn't to rewrite everything for you. The point is to help you become a better writer by pointing out your problem areas so you can fix them.

**Right now, it's just the core features.**

-   Detect AI sentence structures and patterns.
-   Tell the user why it's problematic and actually be useful.
-   Detect reliability issues and highlight them so you can visually see the problem areas.
-   Save and edit multiple documents.
-   Secure Stripe payments for paid subscription.
