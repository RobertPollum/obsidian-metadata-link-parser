# ["Buy Now" Doesn't Work Anymore. Here's What To Do Instead. | by Aphinya Dechalert](https://freedium-mirror.cfd/https://medium.com/@PurpleGreenLemon/buy-now-doesnt-work-anymore-here-s-what-to-do-instead-18245cc9084b?source=rss-3ac3fb3f09e0------2)

Ads are everywhere, but no one is 'seeing' them anymore. They've turned into visual noise that we've learned to block out. On average, we get hit with approximately [1,600 ads per day](https://www.eskimi.com/blog/ad-exposure-research) through social media. How many of them do we actually remember?

Probably zero.

Traditional 'buy now' ads used to be a persuasion tool. Now, they're just considered marketing noise that blends into the background.

Banner ads used to work. [Back in 1997](https://astrad.io/the-rise-of-ad-blindness-and-how-to-fight-i), click-through rates used to hover around 44%. Nowadays, it sits below 0.1%. In part, consumers are overwhelmed by the consistent barrage of businesses trying to get their attention, with [one-third](https://signupdate.co.uk/the-reality-of-digital-fatigue-and-ad-blindness) of internet users opting to use ad blockers.

86% of consumers experience "[banner blindness](https://growthsrc.com/banner-blindness-statistics-studies/)", which means that they don't even mentally register banner ads. For businesses, it means wasted ad budgets on an invisible asset.

### The Funnel Isn't That Linear

There's more than one kind of advertising. The blunt-force sales ad (we're talking "Buy Now", "Limited Offer", "Free Shipping Ends Midnight" promos) has its time and place. It just doesn't belong everywhere.

When something is constantly 'on sale', it devalues the product and cheapens the brand. However, for some, they feel that there's a need to run these promos all the time.

Advertising itself hasn't stopped working. Marketing falls apart when marketers use the same type of advertising for every stage of the customer journey.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/Buy%20Now%20Doesn't%20Work%20Anymore.%20Here's%20What%20To%20Do%20Instead.%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-z2a5nx7R0A.jpeg)

Source: [Pinterest](https://pin.it/2Ld0t3v9D)

Traditional sales funnel treats attention like a straight line. There is an assumption that if you push hard enough, customers will move forward. But the model doesn't reflect how modern attention behaves. Attention is fragmented and people don't move neaty from top to bottom. They sprial in and out, absorbing different messages from different brands and products depending on context, mood, and timing.

It takes a customer anywhere between [6 to 20 engagements](https://pathmonk.com/b2c-customer-journey-touchpoints/) with the product or brand before a purchase is made. If you're hitting someone cold, it can take anywhere between [20 to 50 touchpoints](https://www.emailtooltester.com/en/blog/how-many-touchpoints-before-a-sale) before conversion happens.

The traditional funnel breaks when it tries too hard to close. When every post, every email, every ad screams *buy now,* the more invisible it becomes. It's because we can smell a pitch from three scrolls away. The funnel still works, but only when it's supported by a flywheel that builds trust first and sells later. Without that balance, every "Buy Now" becomes background noise.

### How To Draw Customers In 101

"Buy now" ads should be the last thing a customer sees. Yes, you need a call to action, but you can do better than screaming at your customers to convert.

Not every ad should sell because some ads are meant to start loops or push you through a customer journey story.

#### Start with Context Ads

Context ads is to *spark awareness* without asking for anything in return. They make people pause because they recognise themselves in the story. It's the ad that makes you think, *"that's me,"* not *"that's for sale."* This is where curiosity begins.

**Example: Duolingo's TikToks.** Half of them have nothing to do with learning a language. It's the owl reacting to trending sounds, pop culture drama, or chaotic office moments.

Once someone knows you exist, the goal is to make them remember. This is where UGCs thrive because it's a sweet spot between familiarity without being a sales pitch. Every authentic mention, tag, or repost keeps the brand orbiting in someone's awareness without ever feeling repetitive.

#### Get customers to drive the sales funnel

A customer sharing a quick skincare routine, a TikTok review, or a "day in the life" clip builds more subconscious trust than a thousand polished ads. [UGCs](https://inbeat.agency/blog/ugc-statistics) are 50% more trustworthy and 20% more influential than any other media type. [93%](https://embedsocial.com/blog/ugc-statistics) of marketers also agree that community-driven customer-based content performs significantly better than branded content.

**Example: The Ordinary.** Their products are driven by their users rather than celebrity endorsements.

Once a customer feels invested, they're more likely to hit the 'Buy Now' button than when you were just bombarding them with banners and sales advert.

### People Want More Than Just a Transaction

A discount code might help, but it shouldn't be the only tool used from the marketing toolbox. Customers want to feel like their choice means something because every click, comment, purchase, and interaction with a brand is a small way of reinforcing an identity.

That's why validation has become one of the most powerful marketing currencies. When a brand remembers your preferences, replies to your comment, or makes you feel seen in their storytelling, it triggers the same dopamine response as social approval.

People don't remember the tenth promo code you sent. They remember how your brand made them feel in the moment they decided to trust you. The secret sauce to long-term customer ROI is not constantly pushing for conversion but connection.
