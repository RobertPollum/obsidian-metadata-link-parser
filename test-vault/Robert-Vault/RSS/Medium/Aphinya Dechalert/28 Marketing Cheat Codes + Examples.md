This is a crash course in marketing frameworks — the ideas, technique, examples and questions to ask yourself if you want to implement this for your product or brand.

It's going to be a long one, so I'm not going to hold you up any further with a long intro. Let's get right into it.

### Awareness & Differentiation Frameworks

The glass is not half empty or half full. That's the wrong question. The correct question would be — how do we empty or fill the cup? Awareness and differentiation is all about perspective — how do we gain the attention of consumers by providing them with something borderline unhinged different?

Here are the strategies.

### 1\. The Purple Cow (Seth Godin)

**Idea:** Be remarkable or be invisible.

**The technique:** create something so novel people *talk* about it.

***Example:*** Liquid Death turning water into a punk movement, Oatly using self-aware packaging to mock marketing itself, and Stanley reviving a 100-year-old thermos by making it a lifestyle symbol.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-Vfwq6aIDeA.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What would make my product impossible to ignore in its current category?
-   If my audience stopped talking about me tomorrow, what would disappear from their lives?
-   What's the one detail that makes people stop and say, "Wait, what is this?"

### 2\. Blue Ocean Strategy (W. Chan Kim & Renée Mauborgne)

**Idea:** Compete where no one else is competing.

**The technique:** Redefine your category by eliminating industry norms and combining value in new ways that make old comparisons irrelevant.

**Examples:** Cirque du Soleil blending circus and theatre, Dyson turning vacuum cleaners into design objects, Liquid Death merging FMCG with metal culture.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-Rrx1TbVhOG.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What rules in my industry could I ignore without consequence?
-   If I couldn't compete on price or features, what would I compete on instead?
-   Where is customer frustration highest and how could I flip it into delight?

### 3\. Category Creation

**Idea:** Don't fight for market share, name a new market.

**The technique:** Coin new language or positioning that reframes what you do as the first of its kind, making competitors look outdated by default.

**Examples:** Lululemon creating "athleisure," Airbnb inventing "belong anywhere," Liquid Death selling "tallboy water" instead of bottled water.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-8Q8zYyoKIe.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What label could I invent that makes my offer sound like a new category?
-   How can I describe my audience so specifically it excludes everyone else?
-   What feels obvious to me but invisible to the market?

### 4\. The Hook Model (Nir Eyal)

**Idea:** Build products people use out of habit, not hype.

**The technique:** Use the loop of Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment to create repeat engagement that strengthens with every interaction.

**Examples:** TikTok's infinite scroll, Duolingo's streaks, Notion's template ecosystem.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-KgbklYWpT5.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What external or internal trigger brings users back to me naturally?
-   What unpredictable reward keeps them curious or satisfied each time?
-   How can I make users invest effort or data that increases their attachment?

### 5\. The Golden Circle (Simon Sinek)

**Idea:** People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it.

**The technique:** Start all messaging and branding from purpose (why) before explaining process (how) and product (what), so belief comes before features.

**Examples:** Apple leading with creative empowerment, Patagonia emphasizing environmental activism, TOMS tying purchases to social good.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-E7kf3KnjOy.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Why does my brand exist beyond making money?
-   Can someone repeat my "why" after hearing it once?
-   How does my purpose influence every part of the experience?

### 6\. Jobs to Be Done (Clayton Christensen)

**Idea:** People don't buy products, they hire them to do a job.

**The technique:** Identify the emotional, social, and functional jobs your customers need done, and design your messaging around fulfilling that outcome.

**Examples:** Nike helps people feel like athletes; Headspace hires meditation to reduce stress; Uber hires for freedom and reliability.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-8jo4XnVabx.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What job does my audience actually hire my product to do?
-   What emotional result do they expect after using it?
-   How do I show that I understand the struggle behind that job?

### 7\. Positioning Map (Ries & Trout)

**Idea:** If you're not positioning your brand, your audience will do it for you.

**The technique:** Visually plot yourself against competitors on key attributes like price, innovation, simplicity, or luxury to identify gaps and clarify your stance.

**Examples:** Tesla positioned between innovation and luxury, Canva between simplicity and accessibility, Ryanair owning cheap and no-frills.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-hcxWNyMRT7.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What two attributes define my market's mental landscape?
-   Where am I currently positioned and where do I want to be?
-   Who owns the spot I'm subconsciously competing with?

### 8\. StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller)

**Idea:** You're not the hero, your customer is.

**The technique:** Structure your brand story so your customer faces the problem, your brand guides them, and success is defined by their transformation.

**Examples:** Apple casting users as creators, Airbnb centering travellers as explorers, Nike making every campaign a personal triumph story.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-FiqZTpRQRL.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What's the real problem my customer needs to overcome?
-   How does my brand act as a guide, not the star?
-   What does success look like for them and how do I show it?

### Growth & Conversion Frameworks

Growth and conversion frameworks explain how attention turns into action. They focus on the psychology of decision-making, showing what keeps someone moving forward and what makes them stop. These models help brands design experiences that feel natural to commit to, where curiosity becomes intent and intent becomes measurable growth.

### 9\. AIDA Funnel

**Idea:** Move people from awareness to action through a structured emotional sequence.

**The technique:** Guide attention, build interest, create desire, and close with a clear action so each stage flows into the next.

**Examples:** Apple product pages moving from visuals to features to lifestyle appeal to purchase, Nike ads progressing from story to motivation to gear.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-8LTsZD9qwS.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Where in my funnel does attention drop off first?
-   What emotion do I need to create before asking for action?
-   Does my message guide people through all four stages or skip ahead?

### 10\. Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

**Idea:** Growth is a repeatable loop, not a straight line.

**The technique:** Track Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, and Revenue to find the weakest link and fix it before scaling.

**Examples:** Dropbox rewarding referrals, Slack tracking activation through first messages, Spotify measuring retention through playlist creation.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-baiY0hqTcC.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Which of the five AARRR stages is underperforming?
-   What event signals that a user has truly activated?
-   How can I make retention and referrals strengthen each other?

### 11\. The Marketing Flywheel (HubSpot)

**Idea:** Growth accelerates through momentum, not linear funnels.

**The technique:** Attract, engage, and delight customers in a cycle that builds speed as energy is added and friction is removed.

**Examples:** HubSpot's inbound model, Figma's shared design ecosystem, Morning Brew's reader referral system.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-20sxYNnXqd.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What activities keep the flywheel spinning after purchase?
-   Where does friction slow the cycle and how can I reduce it?
-   How can my customers fuel the next round of growth?

### 12\. Growth Loops

**Idea:** Each new user should create the next.

**The technique:** Build feedback systems where actions like sharing, creating, or inviting automatically expand reach and value.

**Examples:** Notion templates shared publicly, Canva designs posted on social media, YouTube recommendations creating endless watch time.

**Questions to ask:**

-   What user action naturally attracts new users?
-   How can I turn that action into a visible growth loop?
-   Does my product get better as more people use it?

### 13\. The Fogg Behavior Model

**Idea:** Action happens when motivation, ability, and trigger align at the same time.

**The technique:** Simplify actions so they require less effort, or increase motivation when the action is harder to complete.

**Examples:** Amazon's one-click checkout, Duolingo's streak reminders, Headspace's short guided sessions.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-alquu5kjR3.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Is the action too complex or too easy to feel rewarding?
-   What trigger fits the user's timing or mindset best?
-   Am I increasing motivation or reducing friction?

### 14\. Conversion Rate Optimization Pyramid (Peep Laja)

**Idea:** Optimize in order of importance, starting with relevance, then value, then clarity and friction.

**The technique:** Address why people should care before refining how they take action to ensure emotional alignment before usability tweaks.

**Examples:** Shopify focusing on business outcomes before features, Netflix highlighting content value before free trial prompts.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-GZaVGtJzC7.png)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Is my offer relevant to what visitors want right now?
-   Have I clearly explained the value before showing a CTA?
-   What causes hesitation or confusion in my experience?

### 15\. Cialdini's Principles of Persuasion

**Idea:** People rely on predictable psychological cues when deciding what to trust or buy.

**The technique:** Use reciprocity, scarcity, authority, consistency, liking, and consensus to create credibility and urgency in an ethical way.

**Examples:** Social proof through reviews, limited-edition drops, endorsements from credible experts.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-LbpgPz7lOR.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Which persuasion principle fits my brand naturally?
-   Am I using influence transparently or manipulatively?
-   Which principle reduces hesitation for my audience the most?

### 16\. The Zeigarnik Effect

**Idea:** People are driven to complete what feels unfinished.

**The technique:** Create open loops, visible progress, or cliffhangers that keep attention until the goal is reached.

**Examples:** Netflix's next-episode autoplay, BuzzFeed progress bars on quizzes, onboarding checklists that stop at 90 percent.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-88s40T9Khi.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Where can I leave something intentionally open to create momentum?
-   Does my experience encourage users to finish what they started?
-   How can I visualize progress to make completion satisfying?

### Brand & Perception Frameworks

Brand and perception frameworks shape how people *feel* about a business long before they decide to buy. They deal with identity, meaning, and emotional alignment, defining what your brand stands for and how it's interpreted in culture. These models turn logos and taglines into signals of belonging, trust, and taste.

### 17\. Brand Archetypes (Jungian Model)

**Idea:** Every brand embodies a universal human archetype that shapes its personality and emotional connection.

**The technique:** Identify which of the twelve archetypes such as Hero, Creator, Explorer, or Sage best fits your brand and align your communication, visuals, and behavior with that identity.

**Examples:** Nike as the Hero, Lego as the Creator, Patagonia as the Explorer, Google as the Sage.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-Uo58TvD8uA.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Which archetype best reflects the deeper story behind my brand?
-   Does my brand voice and imagery consistently express that archetype?
-   What emotion should my audience feel when they think of me?

### 18\. Brand Identity Prism (Kapferer)

**Idea:** A brand's identity is made up of six connected dimensions that define how it is seen and experienced.

**The technique:** Balance Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, and Self-Image so internal values and external perception stay consistent.

**Examples:** Apple's minimalist Physique and innovative Personality, Harley-Davidson's rebellious Culture and Relationship, Dove's emphasis on Reflection and Self-Image.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-zouyqWAAow.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Which side of the prism is strongest and which needs work?
-   Do my internal values match how my audience sees me?
-   How can I express all six dimensions through content and design?

### 19\. Gestalt Principle of Design

**Idea:** People naturally group separate elements into patterns that feel whole and unified.

**The technique:** Apply visual principles like proximity, similarity, closure, and continuity so your design and messaging appear intuitive and cohesive.

**Examples:** Apple's product harmony and visual rhythm, Airbnb's clean and unified layout, Nike's balance between imagery and typography.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-8auTWNHPZc.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Does my brand identity feel cohesive or fragmented?
-   How can I use repetition, balance, or spacing to improve perception?
-   Are my visuals guiding the viewer's focus in a natural way?

### 20\. Lovemarks (Kevin Roberts, Saatchi & Saatchi)

**Idea:** Brands evolve from loyalty to love when they inspire emotional devotion.

**The technique:** Build attachment through mystery, sensuality, and intimacy so people connect with your brand beyond the product.

**Examples:** Coca-Cola's nostalgic emotion, Chanel's sensory storytelling, Lego's shared creative experience.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-SNkP0MyNuc.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What emotion does my brand evoke when people interact with it?
-   How do I build intimacy through story, design, or experience?
-   Would my audience genuinely miss me if I disappeared?

### 21\. The Three Ps of Differentiation

**Idea:** Every brand stands out through its product, process, or personality.

**The technique:** Lead with one or two Ps that set you apart and reinforce them across all touchpoints while maintaining strength in the third.

**Examples:** Apple leading with product, McDonald's mastering process, Duolingo standing out through personality.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-60BZpzQJFC.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   Which P truly defines my differentiation?
-   Can someone identify my advantage in a few seconds?
-   How can I make my strongest P impossible to overlook?

### 22\. Tribe Theory (Seth Godin)

**Idea:** People follow communities that share their values, not companies that sell to them.

**The technique:** Build a tribe around a shared belief or identity, create rituals that reinforce belonging, and empower members to lead the conversation.

**Examples:** CrossFit's community-driven identity, Glossier's peer-led marketing, Harley-Davidson's loyalty culture.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-Xl6nP1YJm2.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What belief unites my audience beyond my product?
-   What symbols or rituals can express that sense of belonging?
-   How can I turn followers into advocates who lead the tribe?

### 23\. Cultural Strategy (Douglas Holt)

**Idea:** Brands grow when they align with or resolve a broader cultural tension.

**The technique:** Identify contradictions within culture and position your brand as the answer or catalyst that helps people make sense of change.

**Examples:** Dove challenging beauty norms, Nike supporting social activism, Ben & Jerry's integrating social justice into its brand story.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-2aySqqXbKj.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What cultural shift or tension does my audience care about?
-   How can my brand speak to that issue authentically?
-   Am I reacting to culture or helping shape where it goes next?

### Retention, Loyalty & Behavior Frameworks

Retention and loyalty frameworks focus on what keeps people coming back after the first purchase. They explore how emotion, psychology, and habit create attachment that outlasts discounts or ads. These models show how to design experiences that reward consistency, deepen connection, and make customers feel like part of something they helped build.

### 24\. The IKEA Effect

**Idea:** People place more value on things they helped create or customize.

**The technique:** Involve customers in shaping the product or experience so they feel a sense of ownership and pride in the result.

**Examples:** Build-your-own meal kits, Nike's custom shoe designer, community-driven software like Notion templates.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-SDdbdEDmlJ.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   How can I give customers a hand in creating or personalizing what they buy?
-   What small effort can increase their sense of ownership?

### 25\. The Endowment Effect

**Idea:** People value what they already possess more than what they could gain.

**The technique:** Let customers experience ownership before purchase through trials, previews, or personal progress that feels earned.

**Examples:** Free trials with saved data, Spotify Premium's temporary access, Figma letting users build projects before payment.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-GyQoZkTYxG.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   How can I make people feel like my product is already theirs?
-   What can I give them to personalize or save during a trial?
-   How do I make the transition from free to paid feel like keeping something, not losing it?

### 26\. Loss Aversion (Prospect Theory)

**Idea:** The fear of losing something is more motivating than the chance of gaining it.

**The technique:** Frame value around what customers might miss out on or lose if they delay, rather than what they could gain by acting.

**Examples:** Limited-time discounts, expiring bonuses in loyalty programs, early-access sign-ups for exclusive products.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-3FNWNUJqCu.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What does my audience risk losing by not taking action now?
-   How can I highlight that loss without using fear tactics?
-   Where can I make scarcity feel natural instead of manufactured?

### 27\. The Peak-End Rule

**Idea:** People remember the best moment and the final moment of an experience more than the average of it.

**The technique:** Design a clear emotional high point and a satisfying ending that reinforces positive memory and future return.

**Examples:** Disney parks ending with fireworks, Airbnb thank-you messages post-stay, subscription boxes ending each cycle with a surprise item.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-f3QaSHAZxv.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What is the emotional peak of my customer journey and is it strong enough to remember?
-   How does my experience end and what impression does that leave?
-   How can I make the final moment reinforce trust and excitement to return?

### 28\. The Reciprocity Loop

**Idea:** People naturally want to return a favor when they receive something of value first.

**The technique:** Offer genuine value upfront through content, gifts, or experiences that create a sense of goodwill and connection before asking for anything.

**Examples:** Free educational resources from brands like HubSpot, personalized thank-you notes in packages, loyalty perks given before purchase.

![None](Robert-Vault/ReadItLater%20Inbox/assets/28%20Marketing%20Cheat%20Codes%20+%20Examples%20%20by%20Aphinya%20Dechalert-AroCrlVAyw.jpeg)

**Questions to ask:**

-   What can I give away that builds genuine trust before a sale?
-   How can generosity become part of my customer journey?
-   Does my audience feel appreciated or simply marketed to?
