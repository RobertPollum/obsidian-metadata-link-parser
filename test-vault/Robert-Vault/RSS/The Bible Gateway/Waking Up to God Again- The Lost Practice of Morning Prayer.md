The other morning, I didn’t want to wake up.

The alarm went off. My husband shot out of bed with his usual enthusiasm for the morning, while I didn’t even attempt to open my eyes. A few minutes later he brought up my coffee, as he often does, and whispered, “It’s time to begin our morning with God.”

I grunted. I turned over. I was determined to ignore the coffee lovingly made for me.

This, after writing a whole book about morning prayer. This, after telling people again and again how the practice has changed my life.

Waking up isn’t always easy, especially in the winter months. Living in Chicago in the winter means short, gloomy days and long, dark nights. The cold sinks into your bones. The sun seems to forget to show up. And on mornings like this, I can forget too. I can forget why I wake up to pray. I can forget that I need the presence of God more than I need the comfort of a warm blanket.

But after a few minutes of Jeff’s gentle persistence, I finally stumbled out of bed. I sat on the couch, the same spot I always sit, in front of the fireplace. I held the warm mug in my hands. And I began with God.

Why? Because for me, this is a matter of life or death.

Not because I need God to give me sustenance, but because He is my sustenance. Not because I need God to give me water, but because [He is my living water](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/topics-themes/who-is-jesus/water-of-life-john-4/). Not because I need God to give me bread, but because He is my daily bread.

## The Winter Before the Morning

There was a time when I didn’t begin my day with God. It was another kind of winter. A winter of the soul.

I woke up haphazardly. I reached for my phone. I let the drama that leaked from my handheld device set the tone for my heart. The news [told me what to fear](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/voices/the-pour-over-christian-news/). Social media told me who to compare myself to. Emails [told me what to carry](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/biblical-living/spiritual-warfare/submit-thoughts-christlike-thinking/).

And all of it shaped me. All of it discipled me. All of it set my intentions for the day.

Here’s what I had to learn the hard way: we are being formed every morning whether we realize it or not. The question isn’t if something will shape us in those first waking hours. The question is simply: by what?

[![](https://i0.wp.com/staticblog.bgcdn.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/23163713/greatmorning.png?resize=696%2C86&ssl=1)](https://pages.faithgateway.com/the-great-morning-revolution-obs-registration/?utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=greatobs&utm_id=greatobs)

No wonder my spirit felt numb. No wonder I felt directionless. In the quiet, tender hours when my mind and soul were a blank canvas, I allowed anything but God to meet me. My phone was receiving my first fruits, not the One who made me.

That season taught me something I now believe with my whole heart: **whatever meets you first forms you most.**

## The Bible’s Witness to Morning Prayer

When you slow down and pay attention, morning prayer pulses through Scripture like a heartbeat.

David knew this. “In the morning, Lord, you hear my voice,” he prayed. “In the morning I lay my requests before you and wait expectantly” ([Psalm 5:3](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Psalm%205%3A3&version=NIV&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=waking-up-to-god) NIV).

Notice what David brings to the morning: not anxiety, not hurry, not the weight of yesterday’s failures. Expectancy. He believed something good was waiting for him in the presence of God.

This same rhythm echoes across the whole biblical story.

God calls Moses up Mount Sinai in the morning to renew the covenant. The prophets remind us that God’s mercies “are new every morning” ([Lamentations 3:23](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Lamentations%203%3A23&version=NIV&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=waking-up-to-god)). And Jesus himself, the One who had every reason to sleep in given the crowds constantly demanding His attention, “very early in the morning, while it was still dark, got up … and went off to a solitary place to pray” ([Mark 1:35](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Mark%201%3A35&version=NIV&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=waking-up-to-god)).

If Jesus needed to [begin with the Father](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/voices/why-jesus-prays/), how much more do we?

Morning has always been a theological symbol. It is the time when God restores, reminds, renews, and redirects His people. It is the daily resurrection, the small picture of the great awakening to come.

## What Happens When We Wake Up to God Again

Morning prayer is not about performing spirituality. It’s about opening our hearts to receive God before anything else crowds in.

When we give God our first attention, something shifts. Our desires begin to realign. Our fears lose their grip. Our minds quiet long enough to actually listen. Our hearts soften toward the people we’ll encounter that day. And our spirits awaken to a presence that was there all along. We just hadn’t made room to notice.

In the stillness of morning, we become attentive to the One who does not rush us, overwhelm us, or demand performance. God meets us with mercy. God speaks with tenderness. God orients us before the world disorients us.

Morning prayer forms us slowly, like sunrise. You don’t see the light change all at once. But it changes everything.

## Waking Up Together

Morning prayer is an ancient rhythm, and yet somehow many of us have lost it. We’ve lost the sacred slowness. We’ve lost the expectancy. We’ve lost the simple, steady awakening to God’s presence before anything else claims our attention.

But it isn’t lost forever. We can wake up to God again. I wrote _[The GREAT Morning Revolution](https://faithgateway.com/products/the-great-morning-revolution-daily-spiritual-practices-for-meaningful-moments-with-god?variant=43420381937800&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=greatobs&utm_id=greatobs)_ because I believe we can recover this together. Not as another [spiritual to-do list](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/topics-themes/commentaries/doing-spiritual-disciplines-wrong/), but as a gentle returning to what the saints have always known.

So here’s my invitation: tomorrow morning, before you reach for your phone, reach for the One who reaches for you. Let God have the first word. Let mercy meet you before the chaos does.

Morning by morning. Mercy by mercy. Breath by breath.

May the God who makes all things new meet you in the quiet. And may you find, as I have found again and again, that He was already there, waiting.
