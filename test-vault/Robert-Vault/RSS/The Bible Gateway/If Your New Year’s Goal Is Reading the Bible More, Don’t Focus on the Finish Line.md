At the start of a new year, it is exciting to make goals. Let’s be honest, it feels good! It can also feel like it’s something I _ought_ to do. Every year at the beginning of the new year it feels like everywhere I look I’m being told to “be more” and “do more” and that I need to set lots of New Years goals. (They probably aren’t wrong.)

But when setting goals, most people have an end result in mind, then plan to establish new habits to reach that goal. Maybe it’s going to that new workout class or starting something hard — but typically we set goals with the end in mind. Maybe it’s a number, a status, or a physical race to run. Whatever it is, typically the motivation of a goal is a clear finish line (whether physical or metaphorical).

When it comes to Bible study habits, I don’t think that we should focus on a “finish line.”

## The Problem With Bible Study Goals

Don’t get me wrong — [Bible study goals](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/bible-101/bible-study-tips/read-the-bible-in-a-year/) are great! It’s not only the best area of life to exercise discipline but it’s also the most life changing. Scripture says the Bible is alive and active ([Heb. 4:12](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Hebrews%204%3A12&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=reading-bible-finish-line)), so it is going to work on us and transform everything we do. Being in the Word more this year is a great goal to have!

The problem is that while it is the most important goal we can set, it is also the _least measurable_. You can study the Bible like a school textbook for thousands of hours and not grow in your walk with the Lord if it never moves to [transform your heart](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/topics-themes/character-of-god/holy-spirit-scripture-engagement/).

[![](https://i0.wp.com/staticblog.bgcdn.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/19100025/BG_New-Year-2-728x90-Banner.png?resize=696%2C86&ssl=1)](https://www.biblegateway.com/plus/?utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=reading-bible-finish-line)

So, how do we set a Bible study goal that isn’t just about checking a box but actually, truly, changes our lives? The answer is simple: it isn’t _really_ about Bible study. It’s God study.

I know that was cringey. Hold with me for a second.

We aren’t just called to read the words on the page, but be transformed by the Word. [Colossians 3:16](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Colossians%203%3A16&utm_source=bg&utm_medium=bglearn&utm_campaign=reading-bible-finish-line) tells us to let the Word of God dwell in us. Not master it, but let _it_ master _us_. It was never about accomplishing a reading plan, but always about the act of saying “yes, Lord” and taking a step of faith.

Simply put, God wants our worship — not just our work.

God wants to meet our desires. He wants to be where we run with heartbreak, excitement, joy, and fear. God wants to be so much more than a box to check. **We aren’t meant to study the Bible to check the box, but study the Bible in a way that leads to a heart posture of worship!** Reading the Bible should show us more about our Father and propel us to worship.

## Getting From Study to Worship

If your goal this year is to read more of the Bible, it’s important to understand what this book is. **The Bible is the story of God redeeming His people for His glory.**

Read that again. It’s a [book about Him](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/bible-101/bible-study-tips/one-simple-framework-to-love-bible-study/).

So often we cheapen Scripture into just a moral code of conduct. We act as if Scripture is just about what we should and shouldn’t do. We read the Bible so we know what job to take, who to marry, or how to face our trials and doubts. But that’s taking a book about God and making it about ourselves.

If you want a Bible study that changes your life, look at how Scripture declares truths about God — and then live off those truths. Through seeing God’s actions in dealing with unfaithful Jacob or his mercy dealing with Peter, we see his great wisdom and love — and that informs the decisions we make and reshapes the struggles we face. But it all starts with seeing who God is in Scripture and then living _changed by that_.

The change starts with our understanding of Him and how He works. His sovereignty changes our fears. His love changes our insecurities. His passionate pursuit changes our idols. The God of Scripture is a God who meets us in every single life circumstance saying, “I am everything you are searching for.” But too often we are so focused on ourselves that we miss _Him_.

So, no matter what Bible study goal you set this New Year, ask yourself how it will be worshipful. How will you live out what you are learning? Will you have a journal to write prayerful responses back to God? Where will you put to action what you learn? Where will you [serve Christ’s bride](https://www.biblegateway.com/learn/topics-themes/character-of-god/ten-commandments-wedding-vows/) this year?

## This Should Set Us Free

Earlier I said the problem with Bible study goals is that they’re not measurable. But actually, the _best part_ of all this is that it’s not measurable! There’s no checking-the-box mentality in worship. There’s no end or “completion,” only a joyous invitation.

While so many of us set New Year’s resolutions with the end goal in mind, this is one you get to set that doesn’t just pay off in the end. It pays off every single day. The payoff actually can be hour to hour and minute to minute!

The goal is the journey. You won’t finish this year and say, “Now I’m all done with the Bible!” Rather, you’ll continue on this beautiful life of worshipful response to the Lord. You’ll continue in a life of feasting on the Word of God and the life-changing truths found there.

So, as we begin a new year, set those Bible study goals — but not with a finish-line in mind. Set them with a _lifestyle of worship_ in mind. The beauty in this is that it’s a goal you can’t get to the end of the year and say you failed, because it is a goal you can “reach” any and every day.

Feast on God. Live transformed by that. Repeat tomorrow.

Praise the Lord this isn’t like every other goal in life! In every other area of life we are constantly asked to be more, do more, show up more, even relax more. The Lord doesn’t ask for a performance. Remember, this is worship, not work.

Praise the Lord that the most important goal you can set this year is one that’s dependent on Christ’s work, not our own. We just get to respond to Him! We get to live transformed by His enough-ness. We don’t have to be enough or do more — He is more than enough for us. So yes, read the Bible more this year. But do it not for an end goal, but for the goal of worship!
